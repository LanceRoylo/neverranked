Your clicks are down.

What this page is for: if you’ve already noticed the divergence in your own Search Console and want to understand what changed, what to measure now, and what to do about it, this is the short version. If you have not yet looked at the 90-day chart, do that first. The pattern is more useful than any framing we can write.

What you are looking at

Open Google Search Console. Pull the last 90 days. For most businesses we have measured, the chart now has two lines moving in opposite directions: impressions trending up, clicks trending down or flat. The gap between them is what AI Overviews, Perplexity, ChatGPT, Gemini, Microsoft Copilot, and Claude are now answering directly. Your site is still being seen by Google. It is just no longer where your customer ends up.

This is not a tracking issue. It is not a Google algorithm penalty. It is not seasonality. It is a structural shift in how search results render. AI tools now answer more questions inline. Fewer of those answers send the customer through to a website. The impressions still register because the AI is still pulling from indexed pages. The clicks do not register because the customer got their answer before they had a reason to click.

The honest read on what changed

You are losing the conversion surface, not the visibility surface. Google still knows you exist. AI tools still see your content. Your customer just no longer has a reason to leave the AI answer and visit your site, because the AI already answered the question.

That changes which numbers matter. Three specific shifts:

  • Click-through rate stops being a useful KPI. CTR drops as inline AI answers grow. That drop says nothing about whether you are still being read, recommended, or mentioned by AI. It only says fewer people are clicking through to your site after seeing an answer.
  • Impressions stop being a useful KPI in isolation. Impressions can keep climbing while business value collapses, because impressions count being indexed, not being recommended. AI may be indexing you and recommending your competitor in the same query.
  • The new KPI is whether AI tools mention you when they answer. Across which AI tools. For which buyer questions. In which position. Against which competitors. That is the measurement layer this shift creates demand for.

What we measure for you

NeverRanked runs a structured measurement of what seven AI tools say about your business for the questions your buyers would actually ask. The methodology:

  • Seven AI tools per measurement, in two groups: five citation-grade tools that answer from the live web (Perplexity, ChatGPT search, Gemini grounded, Microsoft Copilot, Google AI Overviews) and two model-knowledge tools that answer from what they were trained on (Claude, Gemma).
  • 18 buyer questions per category, with the exact wording locked so every weekly re-measurement compares apples to apples. The questions mirror how real buyers in your category actually search.
  • 3 repetitions per question per AI tool, to separate signal from noise.
  • 3-week kickoff measurement to build a stable baseline before drawing any conclusions.
  • Weekly re-measurement after the kickoff, so you see what moved before any monthly memo arrives.

The method is documented at /methodology/, the question sets are locked by hash so every re-measurement compares the same questions, and every published number is a dated run on the claims ledger. Gemma is open-weight, which means the model itself is independently inspectable.

Honest scope of what this page does not claim

This page is positioning, not proof. The claims here are general patterns observed across the categories we have measured under the same discipline. We do not claim:

  • That the GSC pattern is identical for every business or every category. We claim it is common enough across the businesses we have measured to be worth checking your own data.
  • That changing what is on your site causes AI to cite you differently. Causation requires pre-registered experiments. We measure what AI cites, and we report observed movement honestly. The measurement is the deliverable.
  • That the divergence is permanent. AI tools refresh their indices and training data on schedules we do not control. The pattern may evolve. The discipline is to keep measuring.
