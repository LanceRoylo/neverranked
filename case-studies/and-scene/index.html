01 The Starting Position

A beautiful brand with nothing for the model to read.

And Scene Hawaii is an applied improvisation training company. Their site is editorial, typography-driven, voice-aligned. Real testimonial from Bank of Hawaii. Two pricing tiers published openly. Founders named. Mouthwash-inspired design that holds up next to any agency portfolio in the country.

And when we checked the source, AI engines had nothing structured to ingest. No Organization. No LocalBusiness. No Service. No Person nodes for the founders. No Review schema for the Bank of Hawaii quote that's already on the page. The brand existed beautifully for humans and invisibly for the models that increasingly answer "best corporate training Honolulu" before the human ever clicks.

The site already had robots.txt and sitemap.xml. Title and meta were decent. The brand voice was strong. What was missing was the layer that tells an AI engine the page is about a Honolulu-based corporate training company called And Scene, founded by Carl Anderson and Lance Roylo, offering applied improvisation workshops priced at $460 per participant or $6,900 for a day rate, with a published Bank of Hawaii testimonial.

That layer is Day 1 work.

02 What We Shipped

Foundation in the first sitting. Three pages by Day 2.

The Amplify engagement opened with the schema work that takes the site from invisible to legible. By the morning of Day 2, the first three content pages were live, each addressing a query bucket the site previously could not appear in.

Day 1 / Schema Foundation
Organization + LocalBusiness: Legal name, alternate name, primary email, founder array, Honolulu locality with full geo coordinates and operating hours, area-served set to United States so the schema reflects the founders' national engagement footprint without misrepresenting the company as Hawaii-local.
Day 1 / Person Nodes
Carl Anderson and Lance Roylo: Both founders given Person schema with worksFor cross-references to the Organization. Carl's LinkedIn URL added as sameAs so AI engines can disambiguate him from other Carl Andersons in the entity graph.
Day 1 / Service + Offers
Applied Improvisation Corporate Training: Service schema with both pricing offers exposed structurally. Per-participant standard rate at $460, day rate at $6,900 for a group of 15. Audience targeted to HR Directors and L&D Managers. The pricing was already on the page; now it's queryable.
Day 1 / Review
Bank of Hawaii testimonial structured: Therese Dickerson's quote moved from an HTML blockquote to a Review schema with author Person, employer Organization, reviewBody, and a 5-star Rating. The same content. Now an AI engine can cite it as social proof attached to the entity, not as floating prose.
Day 1 / WebSite + WebPage
Site-level structure: WebSite schema with publisher reference. WebPage with about reference back to the Organization. Canonical tag added (was missing). Together these tell the model what the page is, who publishes it, and where the canonical version lives.
Day 1 / Validation + Deploy
Shipped to production: JSON-LD validated, deployed via Cloudflare Pages, verified live on andscenehawaii.com the same session. Eight entities in a single @graph block, cross-referenced. No visible change to humans. A new readable layer for the models.
Day 2 / Methodology Page
/applied-improvisation/: Dedicated 1500-word methodology page leading with the brand's strongest validated line ("we don't teach improv, we teach you everything else") and structured around the five behaviors the methodology trains. Includes WebPage, BreadcrumbList, and FAQPage schema with six AI-citable Q&As covering whether this is comedy, whether it works, the difference from team building, what a session looks like, whether participants have to perform, and when applied improv is the wrong call.
Day 2 / Local Hawaii Pillar
/corporate-training-hawaii/: Owns the local query bucket no competitor was filling. Honest about the founders not being Hawaii natives. Five FAQ entries calibrated to Hawaii buyer questions. Industry-specific positioning across finance, hospitality, healthcare, government, and tech. Bank of Hawaii testimonial reused in a Hawaii-specific context with the named SVP attribution structurally exposed.
Day 2 / Comparison Page
/applied-improvisation-vs-team-building/: Fills a buyer-journey gap nobody locally addresses. Side-by-side comparison covering primary goal, format, deliverable, group size, measurable outcome, failure mode, and price. Pick-by-symptom section gives concrete buyer scenarios for each option. Honest acknowledgement that we do not run team building events ourselves, with referral logic to local providers who do.
Day 2 / Sitemap + Internal Links
Cross-page graph tightened: Sitemap updated with all three new URLs at appropriate priority. Methodology page linked from homepage method section. New pages cross-link to each other and back to the methodology page. The internal link graph now reinforces the entity recognition the schema is asking for.
03 Day 0 Baseline

The before state, locked in.

The afternoon of Day 2, after the schema layer and three content pages were live but before AI engines had time to ingest any of it, we ran a citation tracking baseline across the four major AI search surfaces. Five queries. Four engines. Sixty-nine screenshots. Captured in incognito sessions so personal browsing history could not skew the results.

This is what every future weekly run will be measured against. Anything that changes from here is attributable to the AEO work compounding.

Five queries selected to span the buyer journey: "best corporate training Honolulu" (broad local intent), "applied improvisation training corporate" (methodology intent), "team building workshops Hawaii" (adjacent category), "improv based corporate training" (terminology variation), and "leadership development training Honolulu Hawaii" (executive intent).

The most useful single finding is the cleanest one. ChatGPT's answer to "best corporate training Honolulu" lists nine providers, ranks them, includes ratings and "open/closed" status. None of them are applied improvisation companies. Not And Scene. Not THEY improv. The category itself is currently invisible to AI engines for this query. That is the gap being closed.

04 The 90-Day Roadmap

Foundation. Indexing. Compounding.

Amplify is not a one-week sprint. It is a three-month compounding engagement. The schema layer that shipped on Day 1 is the foundation. Citations follow when the entity graph stabilizes and content gaps fill. Here is the plan, published in advance, so the work can be audited against it.

Phase 1 / Foundation (Days 1 to 7)
Schema foundation (shipped Day 1). Entity registration: Wikidata draft submission, Google Business Profile claim, LinkedIn company page schema linkage. Citation tracking baseline: capture initial state across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews for a defined query set including "best corporate training Honolulu," "applied improvisation corporate training," and "team building workshops Hawaii." Initial content gap audit against the top three competing offers in those queries.
Phase 2 / Indexing (Days 8 to 30)
Three to five content additions filling the highest-intent gaps surfaced by the audit. FAQPage schema layered onto the homepage and any new pages. Refinements to schema based on which queries trigger first citations. Weekly intelligence digest documenting any movement in AI answers, SERP positions, or entity recognition.
Phase 3 / Compounding (Days 31 to 90)
First verifiable AI citations. Each new citation reinforces the entity. Quarterly deep-dive strategy session. Expansion of the sameAs network as additional entity profiles are claimed and verified. Documentation of which schema elements correlated with which citation outcomes, so the methodology gets sharper for the next engagement.

Anyone reading this can come back in 30 days and check what shipped against the plan. That is the point.
