02 Headline finding: the savings page penalty

/personal/savings scores 33 out of 100. The grader's per-node detail explains why. ASB ships seven well-formed BankAccount schema nodes on this page, each scoring 95 (green). They also ship one additional JSON-LD block that fails to parse as valid JSON.

The grader's overall score is weighted as 0.6 × min(node_scores) + 0.4 × mean(node_scores). The min-skewed weighting is intentional: a single broken block can tank citation behavior because AI engines treating malformed JSON as evidence the page is unreliable. The math: 0.6 × 0 + 0.4 × 83 = 33.

The fix. Find and repair the malformed JSON-LD block. The seven BankAccount nodes are fine and should remain. The total work is one developer-hour with a JSON validator. After the fix, this page should jump to ~95 and exit the penalty zone immediately.

Why this is the headline finding. Of all the work in this audit, this is the highest impact-per-hour fix. The ratio of effort (single block repair) to lift (exiting an 18pp citation penalty zone) is the best you'll see anywhere on the site. If ASB does nothing else from this audit, do this.

03 Pages with no structured data at all

Four pages publish zero JSON-LD. Each is a high-LTV conversion surface where the absence of structured data directly limits AI citation potential.

  • /personal/credit-cards — credit card discovery is one of the highest-volume banking queries on consumer AI search. Customers ask "best credit card for Hawaii residents," "ASB credit card options," "Hawaii bank rewards card." Without FinancialProduct or CreditCard schema, AI engines fall back to scraping or recommending competitor cards.
  • /business/checking — small business banking switch decisions increasingly route through AI search. Without BankAccount or FinancialService schema on this page, "best business checking Hawaii" queries surface alternatives.
  • /locations — this is the page that answers "ASB branch near me." Zero schema means the locations page is invisible to local-intent AI queries, which is most of the foot-traffic acquisition path. Each branch should publish LocalBusiness schema with address, geo coordinates, hours, and phone.
  • mortgage.asbhawaii.com — the mortgage application subdomain is where actual conversions happen. Zero schema. For a product line where customer LTV is in the multiple thousands, this is the single largest gap on the site by acquisition value.

Combined fix. Templated JSON-LD per page type. The /personal/checking page already shows ASB's team can ship clean schema; replicating that template across these four pages is a 1-2 day implementation, not a re-architecture.

04 Authority signals are entirely absent

Across all 11 sampled pages, ASB publishes zero Person schema and zero AggregateRating schema. Two implications worth understanding:

  • No Person schema means no advisor authority signal. ASB has named loan officers, branch managers, mortgage advisors, and wealth specialists. These are the humans customers want to talk to about high-stakes banking decisions. AI engines actively use Person schema to surface "who should I talk to about a Hawaii mortgage" results. Right now those individuals are findable by direct name search but are not surfaceable as ASB representatives in answer-engine queries. Bank of Hawaii has begun publishing this; ASB has not.
  • No AggregateRating means external sources control your review narrative. When customers ask AI engines "is ASB Hawaii a good bank" or compare ASB to alternatives, the engines pull rating data from Yelp, Tripadvisor, Google Business Profile, and others. Each of these can have different scores, sample sizes, and reviewer demographics. AI engines split the difference, and the resulting narrative is whichever external source weights heaviest. With AggregateRating schema published on your own site, the rating you stand behind becomes a canonical signal the engines weight.

Implementation note. Publishing AggregateRating requires that the rating be substantiated by reviews on your site or syndicated from an authorized source. This is straightforward for any of the major review platforms ASB already uses, and the rating values used should be defensible to compliance review.

07 Implementation roadmap, sequenced by impact

The fixes below are sequenced by ratio of impact to effort. The savings page penalty fix and the homepage entity correction are at the top because they are the highest-leverage single-day work on the entire site.

Priority 01 · Day 1

Fix the malformed JSON-LD on /personal/savings

One developer-hour. Validate every JSON-LD block on the page; identify the malformed block; either fix it or remove it. Page should immediately jump from 33 to ~95 and exit the 18pp penalty zone. This is the single most leveraged fix on the site.

Priority 02 · Day 1-2

Promote homepage entity from Organization to BankOrCreditUnion

The /business/ page already publishes BankOrCreditUnion correctly. Apply the same identifier to the homepage and /personal/ entity declaration. Below is the minimum viable implementation for the homepage:

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "BankOrCreditUnion",
  "name": "American Savings Bank",
  "url": "https://www.asbhawaii.com",
  "logo": "https://www.asbhawaii.com/path/to/logo.png",
  "address": { "@type": "PostalAddress", "addressLocality": "Honolulu", "addressRegion": "HI", "addressCountry": "US" },
  "areaServed": "Hawaii",
  "telephone": "+1-808-...",
  "sameAs": ["https://www.facebook.com/ASBHawaii", "https://twitter.com/ASBHawaii"]
}
Priority 03 · Week 1

Branch schema across /locations and per-branch pages

Each ASB branch needs a LocalBusiness or BankOrCreditUnion node with full address, geo coordinates, opening hours, and phone. This unlocks "ASB branch near me" queries on every AI engine that handles local intent. Template:

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "BankOrCreditUnion",
  "name": "American Savings Bank -- Honolulu Main Branch",
  "branchOf": { "@type": "BankOrCreditUnion", "name": "American Savings Bank" },
  "address": { "@type": "PostalAddress", "streetAddress": "...", "addressLocality": "Honolulu", "addressRegion": "HI", "postalCode": "...", "addressCountry": "US" },
  "geo": { "@type": "GeoCoordinates", "latitude": 21.3099, "longitude": -157.8581 },
  "telephone": "+1-808-...",
  "openingHoursSpecification": [{ "@type": "OpeningHoursSpecification", "dayOfWeek": ["Monday","Tuesday","Wednesday","Thursday","Friday"], "opens": "09:00", "closes": "16:00" }]
}
Priority 04 · Week 2

Schema for the four zero-schema pages

Apply the same template ASB used on /personal/checking (which already scores 96) to /personal/credit-cards, /personal/savings (after fixing the broken block), /business/checking, and the mortgage application subdomain. Each page should publish FinancialProduct, CreditCard, BankAccount, or MortgageLoan schema as appropriate, plus FAQPage where applicable.

Priority 05 · Week 3

Person schema for advisors and loan officers

For each named advisor, loan officer, branch manager, or specialist on the ASB site, publish a Person node with name, jobTitle, worksFor, and a sameAs link to their LinkedIn profile. This unlocks AI surfacing for "who should I talk to about a Hawaii mortgage" queries and similar.

Priority 06 · Week 4

AggregateRating schema

Wire AggregateRating into the homepage and per-product pages, sourced from a defensible review pipeline (your existing review platform, Google Business Profile, or aggregated from multiple). Compliance-reviewed before deploy.
