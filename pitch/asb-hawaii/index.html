01 What the audit found, page by page

The grader scores JSON-LD completeness from 0 to 100. Above 80 is the green zone (AI engines treat the page as confidently structured). 60 to 79 is gold (deploys, but limits citation potential). Below 60 is the partial-schema penalty zone, where empirical research shows an 18-percentage-point citation penalty versus no schema at all. Here is what asbhawaii.com looks like today:

Page                                                Score    Schema present
asbhawaii.com (homepage)                            85       Organization only
asbhawaii.com/personal/                             85       Organization only
asbhawaii.com/business/                             95       BankOrCreditUnion
asbhawaii.com/personal/home-loans                   75       MortgageLoan partial
asbhawaii.com/locations                              0       NONE
mortgage.asbhawaii.com                               0       NONE

Three observations from this matrix worth sitting with.

One. The homepage and the consumer landing page (/personal/) only publish Organization, while /business/ correctly publishes BankOrCreditUnion. AI engines look at the homepage to define the entity. The inconsistency means engines see ASB as a bank for business queries and as a generic organization for consumer queries. That gap maps directly to where AI traffic is growing fastest (consumer mortgage research, switching banks, opening accounts).

Two. The locations page and the mortgage application subdomain publish no structured data at all. These are the pages that matter most for the queries customers actually use ("ASB branch near me," "Hawaii mortgage rates," "best Hawaii bank for first-time homebuyer"). The marketing site is doing the work of being readable. The conversion surfaces are not. AI engines that try to surface ASB for these queries fall back to scraping or, more often, recommending a competitor that does publish the structured data.

Three. No Person schema anywhere. ASB has named loan officers, branch managers, and advisors. These are authority signals AI engines actively use when answering "who should I talk to about a Hawaii mortgage." Right now those individuals are searchable by name but not surfaceable as ASB representatives in answer-engine results. Bank-of-Hawaii and First Hawaiian both have specific Person schema patterns I can show during the call.

02 Why this matters now (the marketing-side answer)

The buying behavior shift in banking is already happening. It is not a forecast. Three concrete changes in the last twelve months that change customer acquisition for ASB:

  • Apple Intelligence and Perplexity in Safari are default-on for millions of iPhone users in Hawaii. Their first question is often a banking decision. ChatGPT and Perplexity sit in front of the click. Their answers are opaque, which means they will not show up in your Google Analytics. They are real traffic sources you cannot currently measure or influence.
  • Google AI Overviews surface above the blue links for "best Hawaii bank for [thing]" queries. Even when ASB ranks well in the traditional results, if the AI Overview names a different bank, the click happens there.
  • ChatGPT now includes web-grounded search in default responses. Customers researching mortgages, business banking, or switching banks now ask ChatGPT directly. We track this across all four engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude) and can tell you, query by query, who is currently being recommended for ASB's target queries.

The bank-specific implication. Banking decisions are high-LTV. A new mortgage customer is worth multiple thousands of dollars over the loan term. Even a few-percent shift in citation share on mortgage and small-business banking queries probably maps to real revenue. The audit will quantify the citation gap by query, so this stops being a thesis and starts being a number.

Yes. The snippet does exactly one thing: it reads the URL path the visitor is on, matches it against an allow-list of approved pages, and appends a JSON-LD script tag to the <head>. It does not read cookies, does not read forms, does not read DOM contents, and makes no outbound network calls. The full source is public at github.com/lanceroylo/neverranked.
