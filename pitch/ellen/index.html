01 Both tools, in one paragraph each

Seerly is a SaaS dashboard. The buyer logs in, runs visibility scans, sees citation share and sentiment, gets a report, and the buyer (or their team) executes the fixes. Pricing starts at $99 per month for one engine and 25 prompts. Their pitch is "control your brand's AI search narrative" and the product backs it up at the dashboard layer. Best fit: an in-house marketing team with bandwidth to act on findings.

Never Ranked is a service with a tool wrapped around it. The buyer doesn't log in to fix anything. We deploy schemas to their site directly via a snippet, refresh dynamic content (events, products) on a daily cron, generate FAQ and breadcrumb drafts from their actual page content for review, and track citation share weekly across seven engines (the most coverage in market, including Gemma, the only open-weight model in the set). Pricing: $750 one-time audit (credited toward the first month if they upgrade), then Pulse at $497/month, Signal at $2,000/month, or Amplify at $4,500/month for ongoing work. Best fit: a business owner who does not have time to act on a dashboard, or an agency that wants to resell schema work as a margin line.

03 When Seerly is the right call

Genuinely. Recommend Seerly when:

  • The buyer is an in-house marketing team at a mid-size company with the bandwidth to act on dashboard recommendations themselves.
  • The buyer wants a tool, not a service. They want to log in and look at numbers. They will execute fixes through their own process.
  • WordPress is their CMS and they value a native plugin install.
  • They are evaluating the category for the first time and $99 is the budget. $99 buys real visibility data.
  • They specifically want sentiment analysis as a primary surface, not as a side metric.

Saying "Seerly fits this buyer" honestly is what makes the next section credible.

04 When Never Ranked is the right call

Recommend Never Ranked when:

  • The buyer is a business owner or marketing director who already knows their site has gaps and wants someone to ship the fix, not describe it.
  • They run a CMS we can drop a snippet on (any CMS works) and don't want to manage a WordPress plugin.
  • They have inventory that changes (events, products, listings) and need it to stay in sync with their schema without manual updates.
  • They want a clear progress narrative for their team or board. The activity log shows every deploy, every refresh, every alert. Tier-aware ownership badges label every roadmap item as "we ship this" or "you ship this" so expectations are explicit.
  • They want content drafts (FAQ, breadcrumbs) generated from their own page text, not generic templates. Every draft is human-reviewed before it goes live.
  • They are an agency. Reseller economics on Signal give them $1,200 per month margin per client at retail, with the schema work happening on our end. First client closes the gap, the next eight pay for the partnership.
  • They want done-for-you and are willing to pay for the labor that takes.

05 What this looks like in practice (Hawaii Theatre, 24 hours)

Hawaii Theatre Center signed with us April 24, 2026. Their AEO Readiness Score on the homepage was 45 of 100, Grade D. They had no schema markup of any kind on a site that does 80,000 to 100,000 patron visits a year. Five red flags. Zero structured data.

Yesterday, May 1, we deployed 36 schemas in one work session:

  • Foundation. PerformingArtsTheater, WebSite, AggregateRating wired to their actual 4.6 from 700 Google reviews.
  • Inventory. 31 Event schemas, one per active show, scoped to /upcoming-events. Pulled from their public events page automatically.
  • Navigation. 2 BreadcrumbList schemas, scoped per section.
  • Editorial. 1 FAQPage schema generated from their actual homepage content. 8 questions, all factual, all approved by their team before going live.

Score moved from D 45 to A 95 in less than 24 hours. Six of twenty-six roadmap items checked off. Daily Event refresh now runs automatically every morning at 6am UTC. When a show sells out and drops off the page, its schema deactivates the next morning. Their team does nothing.

That is the structural difference. Seerly would have shown them they had 80% visibility. We shipped the fixes. Public proof at github.com/LanceRoylo/neverranked. The actual Event schemas serving live at app.neverranked.com/inject/hawaii-theatre.json.
