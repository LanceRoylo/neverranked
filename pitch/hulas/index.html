4. Your website is already strong

We also scanned hulas.com the way the AI tools read it. It scored 90 out of 100, an A. Someone has done real work here. The site is clean, the structure is right, and the description already speaks the language a searcher uses. This is not a fixer-upper, and we would not pretend otherwise.

Three specific, small gaps the scan did surface, none of them urgent:

  • No review-rating data in the page code. AI tools look for a star-rating signal to cite as social proof. Hula's has the reviews. The site just does not expose them in the format AI reads.
  • Agent readiness is low (45 of 100). As AI shifts from answering to doing things for people, it looks for a clean "contact this business" signal in the code. Yours is missing, so an AI trying to act for a visitor has to guess.
  • No llms.txt file. A small, standard file that hands AI tools a clean map of the site. Easy to add.

The point of listing these is not the fixes. It is that a site scan, even a perfect one, cannot tell you the thing in Section 3. Whether AI names you in the answer is a different measurement than whether your site is well built, and it is the one that decides who the visitor walks in to see.
