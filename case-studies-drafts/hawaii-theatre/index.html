01 The Starting Position

A century-old venue with nothing for the model to read.

The client is a Honolulu performing arts venue. Multi-decade history. Active calendar of touring acts, local productions, and cultural events. Strong brand. Clean site. Real audience. The kind of business AI engines should be confident recommending when someone asks "what's playing in Honolulu this weekend" or "best theatre venue Oahu."

And when we checked the source, AI engines had nothing structured to read. The events page listed 30-plus shows, every one of them with a name, date, time, ticket link, and venue. None of them were marked up as Event schema. No PerformingArtsTheater node identifying the venue itself. No BreadcrumbList helping models understand the site hierarchy. The page was beautiful for humans and invisible to the models increasingly answering "things to do in Honolulu" before a human ever clicks.

The site already had the basics. Title and meta were fine. Sitemap existed. Pages indexed. What was missing was the structured layer that tells an AI engine which performances are happening, when tickets go on sale, who the venue is, and how to navigate the site by category. That layer is the difference between a venue that gets cited when someone asks an AI engine for recommendations, and a venue that does not.

The audit identified the gap on a Wednesday. The deploy started Thursday morning.

02 What We Shipped

39 schemas. Generated, validated, deployed.

Once the audit identified the gaps, the work compressed. Schema generation runs through a 3-pass validation loop (factual grounding against the source pages, tone check, quality grading) before anything touches the live site. Schemas that pass the deploy threshold are pushed via the same one-line snippet that handles all our customers. No CMS access. No dev cycle. No partial-schema citation penalty.

Phase 1 / Venue Identity
PerformingArtsTheater node: Full venue identity with legal name, address, geo coordinates, hours, social profiles as sameAs. Plus a WebSite node with publisher reference and a canonical tag at the root. Two schemas. The foundation an AI engine needs before it can cite any specific event happening at the venue.
Phase 2 / Event Inventory
34 Event schemas: One per upcoming production. Each with name, performer (where applicable), startDate, endDate, location reference back to the venue node, ticket URL with offers price range and availability, eventAttendanceMode set to "OfflineEventAttendanceMode." Generated from the venue's existing events page, validated in 3 passes, deployed in batches.
Phase 3 / Navigation Graph
BreadcrumbList on key paths: Site hierarchy structured for crawlers. Tells the model "this page is under /upcoming-events/ which is part of the main venue site." Two breadcrumb nodes covering homepage and events index. Auto-deployed at generation since the breadcrumbs are deterministically derived from the URL structure (no LLM hallucination risk).
Phase 4 / FAQ Coverage
FAQPage schema covering buyer questions: Hours, parking, accessibility, ticket policies, refunds. The AI-citable answers are the same ones the box office answers on the phone every day. Generated from existing site copy, multi-pass validated against the source content so we never invent a policy the venue does not actually have.
Phase 5 / Quality Gate
Every schema graded before deploy: Required fields present. Recommended fields populated where the source page supports it. Schema.org type valid. Internal cross-references resolve. Schemas that did not meet the deploy threshold were skipped, not deployed at half-quality. The 18-point citation penalty for partial schema is a real measured cost in the citation literature, and we refuse to pay it on a customer's behalf.

04 What's Compounding

The score is the start. Citations are the goal.

The AEO Readiness Score measures structure. Citations measure outcome. The venue is now structurally legible to AI engines. Whether that translates into citations on real prospect queries ("things to do in Honolulu this weekend," "Hawaii Theatre tickets," "best venue for live music Oahu") is what the next 30 to 90 days will show.

Here is what is running automatically while the citation graph rebuilds:

Monthly Visibility Index
Citation tracking across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews, and Claude: Tracked prompts queried monthly. AI Presence Score computed (citation rate, engine spread, prominence within citations, sentiment). Delivered as a PDF report the first business day of every month. The customer can hand the report to anyone (board, partners, curious staff) without explanation.
Real-Time Citation Alerts
Alert on every change: When an AI engine starts citing the venue for a tracked prompt (or stops), an email digest goes out within hours of the next weekly run. Gained citations and lost citations are split into separate sections so the customer always knows what is moving and which engine is moving it.
Variant Impact Attribution
We measure whether the schemas worked: Every deployed schema is tracked as a variant (FAQPage-A, Event-A, etc). When a new variant goes live, the prior one is marked superseded. Citation rate is measured 4 weeks before vs. 4 weeks after deploy, with a 7-day blackout at the boundary, then run through a two-proportion z-test. The customer's monthly NVI shows the lift, the confidence band, and the methodology footnote. We claim significance only at p < 0.05 with at least 20 runs in each window. Below that we say so instead of guessing.
Multi-Pass Validation On Every AI Output
Factual, tone, quality: Every AI-generated change (FAQ answers, schema descriptions, monthly NVI insight, anything else) runs through three validation passes before it touches the customer's site or inbox. Factual grounding catches hallucinated claims. Tone catches AI-sounding phrasing. Quality enforces our standards. The same brand promise made to a $4,500 customer is the brand promise made to a $497 customer. No AI slop. Ever.
