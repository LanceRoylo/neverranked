# For MVNP

## The wholesale partnership

A leave-behind for the May 18 meeting with ASB. Separate from
the ASB audit. This document is about MVNP specifically: what
the NeverRanked reseller partnership looks like for an agency,
the economics, the operational fit, and the Hawaii market
position available to the first agency that signs.

## The opportunity

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is a service line every Hawaii
agency will be asked to deliver inside the next twelve months.
The clients are already asking. Building it in-house means
hiring people who do not yet exist on the open market, buying a
tool stack nobody has built, and writing a methodology nobody
has tested. Reselling NeverRanked means adding a line item to
next month's proposal.

MVNP keeps the client relationship. NeverRanked does the
technical work. The customer never knows we exist unless MVNP
chooses to tell them.

## The economics

Standard 60/40 split, agency / NeverRanked. Monthly billing.
No annual lock. One active client activates the reseller rate.

These terms are published publicly at
neverranked.com/agencies/partner-program/. Same numbers, same
volume tier structure, same grandfathering policy. No private
floor offered to MVNP that another Hawaii agency cannot see.

| Tier | Retail / month | MVNP keeps | NR receives | What MVNP delivers |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Audit | $500 (reseller intro) or $750 direct, one-time | $300 | $200 | Hands client the audit, books a review call |
| Pulse | $497 | $298 | $199 | Forwards monthly Visibility Index PDF under MVNP brand |
| Signal | $2,000 | $1,200 | $800 | Forwards weekly digest under MVNP brand. Quarterly review meeting. |
| Amplify | $4,500 | $2,700 | $1,800 | Forwards weekly digest + content publishing approval (capped at 6 Amplify clients globally, by design) |

The audit credits toward the client's first month of any
monthly tier within 30 days. Five active Signal clients
nets MVNP $6,000 per month recurring. Ten active Signal
clients nets MVNP $12,000 per month recurring. Each client
adds 3-5 hours per month of MVNP coordination time.

## How it works operationally

Lifted directly from the agency partner program documentation
at neverranked.com/agencies, unchanged for MVNP:

1. **MVNP introduces.** You send us a client. We never contact
   them without you.
2. **NR onboards in 48 hours.** Schema, dashboard, citation
   tracking, scan setup, baseline. Client sees MVNP brand.
3. **NR executes the work.** Schema fixes auto-pushed to client
   site. Weekly citation tracking. Quarterly authority audit.
   Content drafts on Amplify. Everything visible in the
   dashboard so nothing is hidden from MVNP or from the
   client.
4. **MVNP forwards the Monday digest.** Plain English,
   forward-ready under MVNP brand. MVNP stays the account
   lead. MVNP bills the client at retail. NR bills MVNP at
   wholesale.

## White-label assurance

MVNP brand on the dashboard. MVNP brand on every digest. NR
never contacts the client. If a client cancels, they cancel
with MVNP, not with NR, because NR was never in the room. If
MVNP cancels the partnership, MVNP keeps the client. The
relationship was always MVNP's.

This is documented operational practice across every agency
partnership NR runs, not a promise specific to this meeting.

## Hawaii vertical exclusivity (first-mover terms)

The first Hawaii agency to sign at three or more active Signal
clients in a given vertical holds a 24-month lockout for that
vertical, renewable.

For a small market like Hawaii, this is structurally important.
Without exclusivity, two Hawaii agencies could end up competing
for the same Hawaii community banking client with the same NR
technical stack underneath. The lockout prevents that. The
first agency in any vertical owns the vertical for the term.

Verticals where exclusivity is currently open in Hawaii:

- Community banking
- Boutique hotels and hospitality
- Law firms and professional services
- Wealth advisors and financial services
- Real estate and development
- Healthcare (when the compliance template ships)

Verticals where Hawaii Theatre is already onboarded but the
vertical is not yet locked:

- Performing arts and entertainment venues

If MVNP wants the community banking vertical locked
specifically, that conversation can happen at the meeting.

## What MVNP commits, what NR commits

**MVNP commits:**

- Introducing clients via the agency partner program
- Forwarding the Monday digest under MVNP brand each week
- Quarterly review meeting with each client (call or in-person)
- Coordinating compliance review for regulated clients before
  schema deploys (banks, healthcare, legal)
- Billing the client at retail

**NR commits:**

- 48-hour onboarding per client
- Schema deployment via the snippet (no engineering on MVNP or
  client side)
- Six-engine citation tracking with statistical attribution
  at p&lt;0.05
- Quarterly drift detection + automatic re-audits
- Compliance-aware Schema.org templates for financial services
  (pre-flighted against FDIC, NCUA, FINRA, Reg DD, Reg E,
  Truth in Savings, Equal Housing Lender review concerns)
- 60% of retail to MVNP, on time, monthly
- Public methodology so MVNP and the client can audit at any
  time at neverranked.com/state-of-aeo
- Direct founder access during onboarding and for the first
  90 days per client

What NR does NOT commit:

- Marketing or creative work (MVNP's domain)
- Account strategy or campaign work (MVNP's domain)
- Customer-facing contact unless MVNP invites it

## The 5-client scenario

Concrete operational picture if MVNP signs five Signal clients
across the next 90 days:

- **Revenue:** $6,000 per month recurring to MVNP, beginning
  in the month each client onboards. After 90 days with all
  five active: $72,000 annualized.
- **Margin:** $6,000 is the gross. Subtract MVNP's
  coordination cost (roughly 15-25 hours per month across
  five clients, billable at MVNP's standard rate or covered
  inside existing retainers). Net margin depends on MVNP's
  hourly rate but typically 60-80% of gross stays.
- **Operational load:** five 48-hour onboardings (spread
  across the quarter), five Monday digests forwarded each
  week, five quarterly reviews. Roughly the operational shape
  of one mid-sized retainer client distributed across five.
- **First-mover benefit:** if MVNP signs in any of the
  Hawaii verticals listed above before another agency, MVNP
  locks that vertical for 24 months. Any subsequent Hawaii
  agency cannot run the same NR stack on a competing client
  in that vertical.

## What to do at the May 18 meeting

Three decision points for MVNP:

1. **Does the partnership shape fit MVNP's operational model?**
   The Monday digest forward + quarterly review fits most
   agency retainer rhythms. Confirming this is the meeting's
   primary purpose.

2. **Which Hawaii vertical does MVNP want to lock first?**
   Community banking is the obvious one given the ASB meeting,
   but MVNP may have stronger relationships in another
   vertical worth locking first. The lockout terms are the
   same regardless.

3. **What is MVNP's compliance review timeline for ASB
   specifically?** Banks require compliance review before any
   schema ships. If MVNP can broker that review process inside
   ASB faster than NR can navigate it directly, the partnership
   accelerates the ASB engagement. If MVNP cannot, ASB engages
   NR directly and MVNP focuses on other clients.

Either path is fine. The audit Mark is taking from this meeting
is on NR regardless of which way MVNP decides.
