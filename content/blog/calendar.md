# Editorial Calendar

Cadence and schedule for the blog. One post per week, published
Tuesday morning Pacific. Batch production once a month: four
posts in a focused two to three hour session.

Update this file when posts are planned, drafted, or shipped.

---

## Cadence rules

- **Frequency:** One post per week
- **Publishing day:** Tuesday morning, 7am-10am Pacific (covers East
  Coast morning + West Coast morning windows)
- **Production rhythm:** Batch four posts on the first Sunday of
  the month. Aim for two to three hours of focused work.
- **Topic mix:** 50% vertical playbook, 30% category education,
  20% conversion / consideration
- **Avoid:** posting two of the same archetype in a row. Avoid
  posting two of the same vertical tier in a row.

## Production workflow

Standard workflow per post:

1. **Pick the next post** from the planned section above. Cross-check
   archetype mix and vertical tier rules.
2. **Pull the template** from `template.md` as the starting point.
3. **Research and source** every claim using `sources.md`. Add new
   sources to that file if you discover them.
4. **Draft** the HTML, matching the structure of an existing post
   like `blog/aeo-for-financial-advisors/index.html`.
5. **Run all three QA layers** in `qa-checklist.md`. Fix until
   every check passes.
6. **Save** to `blog/[slug]/index.html`. Commit with message
   `Blog: <slug> - <one-line summary>`. Push.
7. **Update this file**: move the row from Planned to Posted with
   the live URL and the date.
8. **Update `verticals.md`**: change vertical status to ✅ posted
   if it is a vertical playbook.
9. **Reshare** on social per the social calendar:
   `social/calendar.md`. Each new blog post should generate at
   least one LinkedIn post and one IG post highlighting the key
   insight.

## When the calendar drifts

If a week is missed (no post shipped that Tuesday):

- Do not double up the next week. Skip the missed week entirely.
- Log the miss with a one-line reason: "skipped 2026-05-12, founder
  travel" or "skipped 2026-05-12, batch production behind"
- Get back on cadence the following week

Consistency over volume. A 30-week streak of weekly posts is more
valuable to the AEO category authority signal than 50 posts
shipped in a chaotic pattern.
