# Post 03 — The AEO Scorecard

**Format:** Single 1200×1200 image + caption.
**Audience:** Lance's personal LinkedIn — SMB owners, marketing leads, fellow founders.
**Destination:** Free 30-second check at neverranked.com (URL check on the homepage hero).

## Lever

**Specificity + Demonstration.** The post is the audit, in poster form.

Every claim is anchored in a real moment from Lance's inbox the morning of posting. Real city, real category, real score. The numbers are not invented.

## Visual rationale

The poster mirrors the website's audit visualization (gauge + 8-signal grid + 6-engine strip) so the prospect already knows what they will see when they click through. This collapses the cognitive cost of "what do I actually get?" to zero.

Composition reads top-to-bottom in five beats:
1. Mark + episode label — establishes the series and the brand
2. Section eyebrow + serif headline — earns the scroll
3. Hero sub-line in DM Mono — the demo, told plainly
4. Scorecard panel (gauge + signals) — the proof
5. Six-engine strip + footer — the moat in two words

## Caption rationale

- Opens with a real demo moment, not a stat.
- Names all six engines explicitly to avoid the "four engines" drift that has crept into other captions.
- Educates on the eight-signal mechanic without listing all eight (the visual does that work).
- Soft CTA: "drop your score, I'll reply" — turns the comments thread into social proof and lets Lance triage replies into Pulse / Audit conversations.

## Quality checks

- **Lever Test:** Specificity. Six numerical anchors (3 names, 65/100, 8 signals, 6 engines, 30 seconds, 2 lit baseline).
- **Swap Test:** Sub Profound or Otterly for NeverRanked and the post breaks. They have no public 30-second tool returning a score.
- **Distinctiveness:** Nobody else in the AEO category is publishing the entity-audit grid as a static asset.
- **Voice:** No em dashes. No emojis. No formulaic filler. Engine count is correct (six).
- **Anonymization:** The prospect (Skincare by Alana) is referenced as "an esthetician in Dana Point." Real city, real category, real score, no brand name. Posting a cold-prospect's name publicly would spook the deal.

## Posting notes

- Drop the URL as plain text in the post body. Do not let LinkedIn render the preview card — it suppresses reach.
- Optional: paste the URL again in comment one for the engagement bump.
- Best windows: Tue/Wed/Thu, 8–10am or 12–1pm in the target timezone.
- Hashtags optional, max 2–3. Suggested: #AEO #SEO #AnswerEngineOptimization.
- Reply rhythm: respond to every commented score within 24 hours with one specific fix. Do not pitch in those replies. Just diagnose. Pulse / Audit conversations come from DMs that follow the public reply, not from the public reply itself.
