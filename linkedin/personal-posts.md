## Post 3 — Tactical how-to (Specificity)

Three schema fields that disproportionately move the needle for AI citation.

1. sameAs. Links your entity to its Wikipedia, Wikidata, Crunchbase, and LinkedIn pages. The engines use these to verify you're who you say you are. Missing it is the top reason brands get paraphrased instead of named.

2. author with a populated Person schema. Not just a byline string. AI answer engines weight "who wrote this" as a proxy for whether to cite. A string like "By Jane Smith" is not the same as a linked entity with credentials.

3. speakable. Marks the 2-3 sentences you want read aloud by voice assistants. It's also a strong hint to text-based engines about which part of the page to quote.

None of this is new. It's just unused. I still audit top-3-ranking sites that ship zero of the three.

## Post 8 — Four engines, four problems (Specificity)

Four engines. Four different problems.

Google AI Overviews. Pulling from top organic, but weighting freshness and structured data over backlink profile. You can be #1 and never appear in the Overview.

ChatGPT Search. Cites from a smaller curated set. If your entity isn't well-defined across the open web, you're paraphrased or skipped.

Perplexity. Fastest-moving citation graph. Rewards explicit source linking and well-scoped FAQ content.

Gemini. Heaviest weight on Google's Knowledge Graph. If your Google Business and Wikidata presence aren't cleaned up, you're invisible here.

Most brands I audit are visible on one of the four, kind-of-visible on one, and missing from two.
