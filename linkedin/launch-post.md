## How to post

1. Upload `post-01-scorecard.png` as the image.
2. Paste the caption exactly as written above.
3. Do not drop the neverranked.com URL in a preview card. Put it in the caption body only (LinkedIn's algorithm suppresses posts with link-preview cards reaching out of the feed).
4. Comment 1 (from you, immediately after posting): your real score on Montaic or neverranked.com, as a filled-in screenshot of the scorecard. This sets the tone and shows you went first.
5. Pin the post for one week.

---

## Engagement plan

- **First 60 minutes:** reply to every commenter personally, within 5 minutes. LinkedIn's early engagement window is the whole game.
- **"Roast" replies:** when someone asks for the roast, send them a short DM with three specific findings from a quick scan of their domain. Do not dump a 2,000-word audit. Three bullets, 48 hours.
- **Day 2-3:** repost the best commenter scorecards to your feed with a one-line observation each ("X scoring 3/4 makes sense — they were early on schema").
- **Day 7:** post a roll-up: "50 brands posted their scores. Here's what I learned." This becomes personal post #11 and closes the loop.

---

## Creative levers used

- **Complicity.** We're doing this together, I'll go first, no one is above the scoreboard.
- **Format Surprise.** The scorecard image is a template people can refill. This turns viewers into posters.
- **Specificity.** Four named engines, 30 seconds, three findings, 48 hours. Numbers and nouns do the persuasion.

Passes the Swap Test: a generic agency couldn't run this mechanic because they don't have a public tool that returns a 4-engine score in 30 seconds.
