Six engines. Six different problems.

Google AI Overviews. Pulling from top organic, but weighting freshness and structured data over backlink profile. You can be #1 and never appear in the Overview.

ChatGPT Search. Cites from a smaller curated set. If your entity isn't well-defined across the open web, you're paraphrased or skipped.

Perplexity. Fastest-moving citation graph. Rewards explicit source linking and well-scoped FAQ content.

Gemini. Heaviest weight on Google's Knowledge Graph. If your Google Business and Wikidata presence aren't cleaned up, you're invisible here.

Claude. No live web grounding in the public API yet. It answers from training-data snapshots, which means entity coherence across Wikipedia, Wikidata, and your About-page identity matters more than recency. Contradictory sources get you hedged or skipped.

Microsoft Copilot. Rides on the Bing index. Favors editorial and structured content the way classic SEO agencies were built to optimize for. Hidden upside: if you already win Bing organic, you tend to win Copilot too.

Most brands I audit are visible on two of the six, kind-of-visible on one, and missing from three.
