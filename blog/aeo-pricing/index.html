AEO Pricing: What It Costs and What You Should Get

By Lance Roylo Published May 2026 Read 9 min

Nobody publishes AEO pricing because the market is new enough that agencies can charge whatever they want. There is no established rate card, no industry benchmark, no Glassdoor for what answer engine optimization should cost. That ambiguity benefits the seller, not the buyer.

We publish ours because hiding pricing is a signal that the pricing is not defensible. If an agency will not tell you what they charge until you sit through a 45-minute discovery call, the price is probably higher than the value.

Here is what AEO services actually cost across the market, what each tier should include, and how to calculate whether the math works for your business.

The three tiers

AEO pricing falls into three categories. The distinctions matter because you are buying different things at each level.

Tier 1: One-time audit -- $750 to $2,000

What you get: AI citation share analysis, schema audit, entity consistency check, content gap analysis, competitor comparison, and a 90-day roadmap.

What you do not get: Implementation. This is the diagnosis, not the treatment. Nobody touches your website or creates content at this price point.

Good for: Businesses that want to understand the gap before committing to ongoing work. You walk away knowing exactly where you stand and what needs to happen next.

Turnaround: 48 to 72 hours.

Tier 2: Monthly monitoring -- $1,500 to $3,000/mo

What you get: Weekly automated scans, score tracking, regression alerts, competitor benchmarking, prioritized fix recommendations, and a monthly strategy call.

What you do not get: Someone doing the implementation for you. You still need a developer or agency to execute the recommendations. This tier tells you what to do. It does not do it.

Good for: Businesses with internal marketing teams that need direction. If you have people who can execute but lack the AEO-specific knowledge to prioritize, this is the right level.

Tier 3: Full-service implementation -- $3,000 to $5,000/mo

What you get: Everything in monitoring, plus schema deployment, content creation, entity registration, and ongoing optimization. The agency does the work, not just the recommendations.

What you do not get: A reason to think about AEO internally. This is the hands-off tier.

Good for: Businesses that want results without building internal AEO capability. Most common with service businesses that generate enough revenue per customer to justify the spend.

What drives the price up

Within each tier, the actual price depends on five variables.

  • Site complexity. A 100-page site and a 10,000-page site require different levels of audit depth, schema coverage, and content analysis. Larger sites cost more at every tier.
  • Number of locations. A single-location business has one entity to manage. A franchise with 40 locations has 40 entity profiles, 40 sets of schema, 40 citation sources to monitor. Location count is the single biggest price multiplier.
  • Competitive density. A solo practitioner in a mid-size market faces less competition for AI citations than a personal injury lawyer in Manhattan. Higher competition means more content, more entity reinforcement, and more ongoing optimization.
  • Content gap size. If you need 5 articles to cover your topic authority, that is a different scope than 50 articles. The gap analysis in the audit determines this number.
  • Speed requirements. Standard timelines run 90 days to meaningful results. Accelerated timelines (60 days) require more parallel work and cost 20% to 30% more.

The ROI calculation

AEO pricing only makes sense relative to what a new customer is worth to your business. The math is simple.

Start with your customer lifetime value (CLV). Then divide your monthly AEO cost by that number. The result is how many new customers per month you need from AI citations to break even.

Dentist

Average CLV: $3,000. Monthly retainer: $2,000. Break-even: 1 new patient per month from AI citations. If AEO brings in 2 patients per month, you are running at 200% ROI.

Law firm

Average case value: $15,000. Monthly retainer: $4,500. Break-even: 1 new case per quarter. Most law firms that show up consistently in AI answers for their practice area convert more than that.

Restaurant

Average check: $50. Monthly retainer: $2,000. Break-even: 40 covers per month. This is realistic if you show up in "best restaurants in [city]" answers, which are among the most common queries AI engines handle.

The formula: monthly AEO cost divided by CLV equals the number of new customers needed to break even. For most service businesses, this number lands between 1 and 3 per month. For businesses with high CLV (law, medical, financial services), the math works with a single new client per quarter.

If the break-even number looks unrealistic for your business, AEO is not the right investment yet. If it looks easy, you are probably leaving money on the table by not starting. Most businesses see measurable results within 60 to 90 days.
