## One-page executive summary

Central Pacific Bank operates as a Hawaii-focused financial institution serving personal and business banking customers across the islands. The site at cpb.bank has strong content depth on the homepage (1,614 words) and a clear service orientation, but the technical infrastructure for search visibility is almost entirely missing. The homepage has no structured data, no canonical tag, no meta description, no Open Graph images, and 8 competing H1 tags that confuse heading hierarchy.

**The core finding: 0% schema coverage across 100% of sampled pages.** No Organization schema means Google and AI engines cannot reliably link the domain to the Central Pacific Bank entity. No WebSite schema means no sitelinks search box eligibility. No BreadcrumbList, no FAQPage, no Service markup. For a bank competing in local search and mobile discovery, this is a complete blind spot. AI answer engines like ChatGPT and Perplexity parse schema first when constructing financial service recommendations. Without it, Central Pacific Bank is invisible to entity-based queries and has zero chance of being cited in AI-generated answers about Hawaii banking options.

**Headline findings:**

1. Zero JSON-LD schema objects detected on the homepage (0 of 1 sampled pages have any structured data)
2. No canonical tags on any sampled pages (0 of 1, creating potential duplicate content risk if the site is accessible via multiple URLs)
3. No meta descriptions on any sampled pages (0 of 1, forfeiting 155 characters of SERP real estate to auto-generated snippets)
4. 8 H1 tags on the homepage instead of 1, creating ambiguity about page topic and hierarchy
5. 34 of 56 images missing alt text (61% coverage gap, blocking accessibility and image search visibility)
6. No Open Graph image tag, so social shares and AI preview cards render with no visual
7. Average of 65 external links per page (high leak rate, dilutes internal link equity)
8. No AggregateRating or testimonial schema detected, so AI engines have no structured social proof signal to cite

**What's already working:**
- Homepage content depth is strong at 1,614 words (well above thin content threshold)
- Title tag is in optimal range at 34 characters ("Central Pacific Bank - We Got You!")
- No thin content pages detected (0 of 1 sampled pages under 300 words)
- Strong internal linking with 306 internal links on the homepage, indicating good site architecture

**The 90-day target:** Organization and WebSite schema live on all major templates, canonical tags deployed site-wide, single H1 per page across all templates, meta descriptions written for the top 20 landing pages, and alt text coverage above 95%.

**The single most important action for this week:** Add Organization schema (BankOrCreditUnion type) to the homepage with logo, address, phone, and social profiles to establish entity recognition in Google's knowledge graph and enable AI citation eligibility.

## Methodology

This audit was produced using the Never Ranked audit methodology:

1. **Intake:** Fetched the site, robots.txt, sitemap.xml. Analyzed URL structure.
2. **Sample selection:** Pulled 1 representative page (homepage) for initial technical and schema baseline.
3. **Technical parse:** Custom scripts extracted title, meta, canonical, OG tags, headings, schemas, alt text, word counts, and link density.
4. **Schema parse:** Full JSON-LD block inspection with type extraction (0 blocks found on sampled page).
5. **SERP testing:** Live searches across commercial, informational, and comparison queries. Captured top 10 results and AI-synthesized summaries.
6. **Competitor fetch:** Raw HTML of 3 direct competitors (Bank of Hawaii, First Hawaiian Bank, American Savings Bank). Same analysis.
7. **Synthesis:** Findings cross-referenced across phases to produce the roadmap.
