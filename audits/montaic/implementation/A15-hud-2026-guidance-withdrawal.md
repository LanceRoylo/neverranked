The 2026 Federal Register notice is five days old as of the pivot date. ### The problem the piece describes

On April 6, 2026, the Federal Register published Notice 2026-06624 noticing the withdrawal of Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity guidance documents. The underlying memo, signed by Principal Deputy Assistant Secretary for FHEO John Gibbs, took effect upon receipt on September 17, 2025. Federal Register notice did not appear for 201 days.

Trade press, including NAHRO and LeadingAge, reported the withdrawal as covering eight guidance documents. The actual memo withdraws nine. The discrepancy is the two separate June 2022 criminal records documents (FHEO's application guidance and OGC's implementation guidance) getting counted as one item.

Item #9 on the withdrawal list is Guidance on Application of the Fair Housing Act to the Advertising of Housing, Credit, and Other Real Estate-Related Transactions through Digital Platforms, issued April 29, 2024 and withdrawn less than two years later. That item touches every online real estate listing in the country.

The practical effect is that compliance training materials that cited any of the nine items as authority are now pointing at withdrawn documents. The gap is not a gap in the underlying law. Fair Housing statute and regulation still apply. The gap is that the interpretive layer agents were using to translate statute into practice is thinner.

The piece walks through five things:

1. The April 6, 2026 Federal Register notice and the underlying September 17, 2025 memo. Date mechanics and the 201-day gap.
2. The count correction. Nine, not eight. Which two items trade press conflated.
3. Each of the nine withdrawn items. What it was, what it covered, who cited it, and what statute, regulation, or case law still covers the same territory.
4. A deeper read on item #9 (digital advertising practices) because it touches online listings directly.
5. A short history sidebar: 24 C.F.R. Part 109 (1989 through 1996) and the Achtenberg memo (1995). Context for why HUD's fair housing guidance layer has been narrowing for thirty years, not just this week.

### The Montaic bridge

Montaic screens every generated listing against 42 U.S.C. § 3604(c), 24 C.F.R. § 100.75, and a case law corpus maintained and updated as new decisions land. The product bridge for A15 is not "we adapted to the withdrawal." It is "we never cited the withdrawn guidance as authority, so our compliance layer did not change when HUD narrowed the guidance layer."

The bridge is stronger than any previous Montaic piece because the competitive framing is real. Every compliance vendor that built its rules against HUD guidance documents now has to decide whether to remove the withdrawn items or defend them. Montaic did not cite them in the first place. That is a position no other vendor can claim without rebuilding their rules engine.

The tone stays measured. The piece does not gloat. It names the facts and lets the reader reach the commercial conclusion.

### Blocker 1: Federal Register Notice 2026-06624 and the Gibbs memo. CLOSED 2026-04-11.

**Effort actual:** 1 hour (search plus fetch plus extraction). Budget was 2 to 3 hours.

**Source documents retrieved:**

- HUD memo PDF: `https://www.hud.gov/sites/dfiles/Main/documents/Notice-of-Withdrawal-of-Guidance-Documents.pdf`
- Federal Register listing (confirmed via WebSearch): `https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/06/2026-06624/notification-of-withdrawal-of-fair-housing-and-equal-opportunity-guidance-documents`
- Justia mirror (fallback, 403 on fetch): `https://regulations.justia.com/regulations/fedreg/2026/04/06/2026-06624.html`

**Signing official:** John Gibbs, Principal Deputy Assistant Secretary for Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity, U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development.

**Recipients of the memo:** FHEO, Fair Housing Assistance Program (FHAP) Agencies, Fair Housing Initiatives Program (FHIP) Grantees.

**Date mechanics:**

- Digital signature timestamp on the PDF: September 16, 2025, 14:39:02 EDT.
- Memo dated: September 17, 2025.
- Effective date: "Upon receipt."
- Expiration: "This Notice will remain in effect until amended, superseded, or rescinded."
- Federal Register publication: April 6, 2026 (as document 2026-06624, Vol. 91, No. 65, pages 17291-17292).
- Gap from memo effective date to Federal Register publication: 201 days.

**Authority cited by HUD for the withdrawal:**

- Executive Order 14192 (January 31, 2025), "Unleashing Prosperity Through Deregulation."
- Executive Order 14219 (February 19, 2025), "Ensuring Lawful Governance and Implementing the President's Department of Government Efficiency Deregulatory Initiative."

**Three-criteria withdrawal framework:** HUD states that each withdrawn guidance document failed at least one of three criteria. A document is retained only if all three hold.

1. The guidance is statutorily prescribed.
2. The interpretation therein is consistent with the relevant statute or regulation.
3. The guidance decreases compliance burdens.

**Enforcement posture during withdrawal:** The memo states FHEO "will deprioritize enforcement against regulated parties whose conduct does not conform to the guidance during the pendency of any withdrawal." This is material for the piece because it means agents who relied on the withdrawn guidance between September 17, 2025 and April 6, 2026 are not retroactively exposed for conduct that aligned with the withdrawn rules.

**Reissue posture:** "Such withdrawal is not necessarily final. The Office of Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity intends to continue reviewing all guidance documents to determine whether they should ultimately be retained or revised and reissued."

**The nine withdrawn guidance documents, verbatim from the memo table.**

**The count-correction finding.** NAHRO headline: "HUD Withdraws Eight Fair Housing Notices." Industry trade press coverage has been reporting eight. The actual memo withdraws nine. Items #6 and #7 are both criminal records guidance, both issued in June 2022, but they are listed as two distinct rows in the memo table with different dates. Item #6 is the substantive FHEO application guidance. Item #7 is the OGC implementation guidance that operationalized #6. Trade press appears to have treated them as a single item. The piece surfaces this correction explicitly.

**The Montaic item.** Item #9, "Guidance on Application of the Fair Housing Act to the Advertising of Housing, Credit, and Other Real Estate-Related Transactions through Digital Platforms," was issued April 29, 2024 and withdrawn 17 months later. Of the nine withdrawn items, #9 is the one that sits directly on top of Montaic's customer base. A15 gives this item a deeper read than the other eight.

### Blocker 4: 1989 through 1996 history subsection. CLOSED (findings captured 2026-04-11).

Findings from the 2026-04-11 Phase 2 research are locked in. Full history:

- **January 23, 1989.** HUD published 24 C.F.R. Part 109 in the Federal Register. Formal regulation on fair housing advertising. Included a phrase-level guidance schedule that became the industry reference list.
- **December 28, 1990.** Part 109 amended.
- **January 9, 1995.** Assistant Secretary Roberta Achtenberg issued a memorandum clarifying enforcement priorities under Part 109. This memo is the artifact most often confused for "the 1989 HUD word list" in compliance training materials.
- **May 1, 1996.** HUD rescinded Part 109 via directive FR-4029-F-01. Public, dated, documented. Not "quiet," not "in the 2000s."
- **1996 through 2025.** Fair housing advertising enforcement operated under 42 U.S.C. § 3604(c), 24 C.F.R. § 100.75, and case law. No formal regulation at the phrase level.
- **September 17, 2025 (effective) / April 6, 2026 (noticed).** Gibbs memo withdraws nine remaining fair housing guidance documents, narrowing the interpretive layer further.

The history subsection in the piece is one paragraph. It exists to frame the 2026 notice as the latest step in a thirty-year narrowing, not as a sudden reversal. Keeps the piece from reading as partisan.
