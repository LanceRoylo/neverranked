Why MLS Descriptions Take So Long (And How to Fix It)

The average agent spends 20-45 minutes writing a single MLS description. Multiply that by 10-15 active listings and you are spending a full work day each month on writing alone. That is time you could spend prospecting, showing properties, or closing deals.

The manual process looks the same every time: stare at the property details, try to come up with a compelling opening line, worry about Fair Housing language, check character limits, rewrite twice, and settle for something that is good enough. It works, but it does not scale.

AI changes the math. With Montaic, you enter your property details once and get a polished MLS description in 30 seconds. Not a template. Not a fill-in-the-blank. A description that reads like you wrote it, because the AI learned your voice.

What a Great MLS Description Includes

A strong MLS description opens with the property's most compelling feature, not the address or the bedroom count. Buyers filtered by price, size, and location before they clicked your listing. Your description needs to tell them what the photos cannot.

The body should flow naturally from the exterior to the interior, highlighting the features that differentiate this property from competing listings. Include specific details: the brand of appliances, the type of flooring, the age of the roof, recent renovations with dates.

Close with lifestyle context. How does the home live? What is the neighborhood like? What is the commute to the nearest employment center? These details help buyers picture themselves in the home, which is the point of the description.
