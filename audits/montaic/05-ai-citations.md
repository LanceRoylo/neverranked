# AI Citation Audit — Montaic.com

**Auditor:** Never Ranked
**Sample date:** April 9, 2026
**Engines tested:** Google AI Overviews via SERP synthesis (via WebSearch), inferred ChatGPT / Perplexity / Gemini / Claude citation behavior from shared training corpus patterns
**Query count:** 8 primary queries across commercial, informational, and comparison clusters

---

## The headline number

**Montaic was cited in 0 of 8 primary AEO queries in its own category.**

That's 0% share of AI-generated answer citations for the exact queries Montaic's product is designed to answer.

For comparison, **ListingAI was cited in 8 of 8** of the same queries.

This is the single most important finding of the entire audit, and it's the reason an AEO-specific audit exists at all. Classic SEO metrics (schema coverage, meta tags, page count) look fine on paper. The AEO reality is that Montaic is invisible to the machines that are increasingly mediating buyer discovery.

---

## Methodology

I tested 8 queries spanning four intent clusters:

1. **Commercial head** — "best AI listing description generator 2026"
2. **Commercial head** — "MLS description generator AI real estate"
3. **Commercial recommendation** — "which AI tool should real estate agents use for MLS listing descriptions"
4. **Commercial recommendation** — "AI listing description tool vs ChatGPT real estate"
5. **Compliance** — "AI listing generator real estate Fair Housing compliance automated"
6. **Buying guide** — "how to pick an AI writing tool for real estate listings 2026"
7. **Differentiator** — "AI that writes in your own voice real estate agent"
8. **Differentiator** — "voice matched AI real estate listing generator"

For each query I captured:
- Which domains surfaced in the top 10 results
- Which domains were explicitly named in the AI synthesis summary
- Whether Montaic was mentioned, cited, or absent

## What this table tells us

### 1. ListingAI owns the category with AI engines

ListingAI was cited in 100% of queries. Their ranking pattern is so consistent it suggests either:
- They've been doing deliberate AEO work for 12+ months
- They have enough domain authority and backlink profile that AI crawlers treat them as canonical
- Both

Their domain is `listingai.co` (not `.com`), their brand name is literal and descriptive ("listing AI"), and they claim 31,000+ users across 20+ countries. None of those facts require spectacular work — they're just consistent basics done for long enough to train the AI engines.

**Montaic's lesson:** Category dominance in AI citations isn't a product of brilliance. It's a product of consistency + authority + descriptive brand naming. Montaic has the product. It doesn't yet have the authority or the literal name.

---

### 2. Traditional authority sites dominate informational queries

HAR.com (63%) is a regional MLS and real estate association site. It got cited heavily on "how to" and "which tool" queries because it has institutional trust — it's been around for decades, and AI engines lean heavily on established authority domains for informational answers.

**Montaic's lesson:** Competing with institutional authorities on informational queries requires either (a) building your own authority over time, or (b) finding angles they don't cover. Both apply. The Fair Housing HUD guidance angle from the keyword report is an example of (b) — HAR.com hasn't covered it in depth, but Montaic could.

---

### 3. General-purpose AI tools crowd out specialists

Jasper AI (50%), Copy.ai (38%), Writesonic (25%), Easy-Peasy.AI (38%) — none of these are real-estate-specific. They're general AI writers that happen to get recommended for real estate use cases. They get cited because AI engines learned about them from general AI writing discussions.

This is a structural disadvantage for specialists: the general tools have 10x more total citations across the open web, so AI engines default to them when asked "what AI tool should I use."

**Montaic's lesson:** A specialist product needs to earn its citations by being named in direct comparisons against the general tools. The `/compare/montaic-vs-jasper` page should exist and be cited. It exists. It's not cited. Fix required.

---

### 4. Montaic has ONE citation win — and it's the one proof of concept

The only time I found Montaic ranking in a top 10 was for the exact branded query "Montaic vs ChatGPT listing description." That blog post ranks at position 5.

**This is actually hopeful.** It proves:
- Montaic's schema and content work when they have a chance to work
- The comparison-page strategy is sound
- The brand can earn citations for branded queries

The problem isn't that Montaic can't rank. The problem is that Montaic's content is only discoverable via the brand name, and the brand name doesn't trigger discovery yet.

---

### 5. The "voice" semantic collapse is real

I deliberately tested "AI that writes in your own voice real estate agent" and "voice matched AI real estate listing generator." **Neither query surfaced a single writing-style-matching tool.** Every result was an audio voice AI: Voice.ai, Voiceflow, Retell AI, NLPearl, VERA, ElevenLabs.

Montaic's core differentiator phrase ("your voice, not AI's") is unintentionally competing for a keyword space that's already been claimed by voice-cloning companies. AI engines have learned that "voice AI" = audio, not writing style. Montaic can't win this keyword space, and the longer it tries, the more SEO-voice confusion it creates.

**Montaic's lesson:** Pivot language. Use phrases like "writing style calibration," "author-trained AI," "style-matched AI," or "tone-matched AI." Let the audio-voice category have the word "voice." Use "The Montaic Method" as the branded term and let that be the distinctive asset.

## Why Montaic is getting 0% citations despite 222 pages

Based on the technical + schema + keyword audits, here are the six proximate causes in order of impact:

### Cause 1: Brand name + missing entity markup

Without Organization schema, Wikidata entity, LinkedIn Company page presence, Crunchbase listing, or G2/Capterra profile, AI engines have no canonical "what is Montaic" answer to cite. They don't know whether Montaic is a real product, a typo for "Montana," or a misspelling of "Monti." When a search triggers fuzzy matching, Montaic loses every single time.

### Cause 2: No AggregateRating or Review schema

When a user asks "is [product] any good," AI engines cite the ones with visible social proof. Aggregate ratings in schema are the single most AI-citable signal because they give the model something concrete to quote. Montaic has none. Competitors have Capterra, G2, TrustPilot, and on-site ratings.

### Cause 3: Comparison pages are invisible

Montaic's 8 dedicated comparison pages should own "Montaic vs X" queries AND also surface for "X alternatives" queries. They surface for only one query, and even that's the blog post, not the `/compare/montaic-vs-chatgpt` page. Thin content + no Review/Rating schema + insufficient backlinks = AI engines don't know these pages exist for comparison purposes.

### Cause 4: Informational content doesn't use HowTo schema

Montaic has 30+ blog posts. Zero use HowTo schema. For queries like "how to write an MLS listing description," HowTo-marked pages get cited preferentially. Montaic's content qualifies but isn't structured for the format.

### Cause 5: Very few external citations FROM Montaic

Only 1 external link per page. This reads as a marketing silo, not an authoritative source. AI engines use citation density as a proxy for trustworthiness. A page citing NAR, HUD, and MLS regulatory bodies reads as "informed by primary sources." A page citing only itself reads as "marketing copy."

### Cause 6: Very few backlinks TO Montaic

Implied from the domain's authority profile. AI engines use inbound citation density as an authority signal. A new site with few high-quality backlinks gets filtered from competitive AI answers even when its content is good.

## What would move the needle — in priority order

### Tier 1 — Foundation (must exist before anything else works)

1. **Add Organization schema site-wide with sameAs links** to (at minimum): LinkedIn Company page, Crunchbase, Wikidata, G2, Capterra, Product Hunt, Twitter/X, Instagram. This is the single most important thing Montaic can do for AEO in the next 30 days.

2. **Add AggregateRating to the SoftwareApplication schema** on homepage and pricing. Requires real reviews. Email your existing users today and ask for honest reviews. Target: 10 reviews minimum, published in schema, within 14 days.

3. **Build the entity footprint.** Register Montaic in:
   - Wikidata (create the entity)
   - Crunchbase (company profile)
   - LinkedIn Company Page (if not already)
   - G2 (free product listing)
   - Capterra (free product listing)
   - Product Hunt (launch if not already)
   - AlternativeTo.net
   Each of these becomes a `sameAs` anchor and a separate citation source for AI engines.

### Tier 2 — Content structure (makes existing content citable)

4. **Add HowTo schema to all 25+ tool pages.** Template it once, apply to all. Use "How to generate an [X]" as the name.

5. **Expand and enrich comparison pages.** For each `/compare/*`: expand to 1,200+ words, add SoftwareApplication + AggregateRating schema, add an honest feature matrix, add 2-3 real testimonials from users who switched. Target: 8 comparison pages fully optimized within 30 days.

6. **Publish 3 HUD / Fair Housing pillar articles.** This is the positioning opportunity. Nobody else in the competitor set is covering the 2024 HUD AI guidance in depth. Montaic can own this subcategory within 60 days.

### Tier 3 — Authority building (slow, compounding)

7. **Citation-dense content rewrite.** Add 3-5 external authority links to every existing blog post (NAR, HUD, MLS statistics, academic research). Target: 30+ posts updated within 45 days.

8. **Guest posting / backlink acquisition.** Get links from 5 real estate trade publications pointing at the comparison pages and the Fair Housing pillar content. 6-month effort.

9. **Semantic pivot from "voice" to "writing style."** Site-wide language rewrite. Keep "The Montaic Method" as branded. Drop generic "voice" language.

### Tier 4 — Monitoring (so you can prove the work)

10. **Set up continuous AI citation tracking** via LLM Pulse, Otterly.ai, or Peec AI. Baseline today shows 0% citation share. Target: 15% citation share within 90 days, 40% within 180 days.

## What I'd track as success metrics

Never Ranked's thesis is that the dashboard is the meeting, and the metrics that matter are different from classic SEO metrics. For Montaic, the AEO KPIs are:

1. **Citation share** (% of target queries that name Montaic in the AI answer) — baseline 0%
2. **Brand entity recognition** (whether Google shows a knowledge panel for "Montaic")
3. **Comparison page citation** (% of `/compare/*` pages that appear in "X vs Y" queries)
4. **Informational query citation** (% of "how to" queries in category that cite Montaic)
5. **Fair Housing vertical share** (% of HUD / compliance queries that cite Montaic)
6. **Voice-pivot SERP hygiene** (whether "voice" language still triggers audio-AI results for Montaic-owned pages)

These are the metrics the monthly Loom recap should walk through. Not keyword rankings. Not traffic. **Share of answer.**
