## One-page executive summary

Emanate Wireless invents, engineers, and manufactures real-time location and asset monitoring solutions for the healthcare industry. The company operates out of Hudson, Ohio with a six-person technical team led by founder and VP of Engineering Gary Sugar. The site is currently a single homepage with minimal content and zero structured data infrastructure.

We sampled the homepage and found a complete absence of entity recognition signals. No Organization schema. No canonical tags. No OpenGraph images. The homepage carries 159 words (half the minimum threshold for substantive content), three H1 tags where one is expected, and a 16-character title tag that tells search engines nothing about what Emanate Wireless does. Eight of twelve images are missing alt text. The site is invisible to AI engines and underperforming in traditional search because the foundational hygiene layer is broken.

**Headline findings:**

1. Zero structured data deployed across all sampled pages (no Organization, WebSite, BreadcrumbList, or FAQPage schema)
2. 100% of sampled pages missing canonical tags (1 of 1 pages)
3. 100% of sampled pages missing OpenGraph images (social shares render as blank cards)
4. Homepage has three H1 tags instead of one (breaks heading hierarchy and dilutes semantic signal)
5. Title tag is 16 characters (under half the 30-character minimum, missing all keyword context)
6. Homepage word count is 159 words (thin content, under the 300-word floor for substantive indexing)
7. 67% of images missing alt text (8 of 12 images are invisible to screen readers and image search)
8. No AI engine citations detected for core queries like "healthcare RTLS" or "asset tracking hospitals"

**What's already working:**
- Meta description is well-written, 131 characters, and clearly states the value proposition
- Robots meta allows indexing and following (index,follow is correctly set)
- Internal link structure is healthy (24 internal links on homepage suggest good nav architecture)
- Domain authority foundation exists (site has been live long enough to have established crawl patterns)

**The 90-day target:** Organization and WebSite schema deployed, homepage word count above 800 with entity-rich copy, all canonical and OpenGraph tags in place, and first AI engine citation captured for a core RTLS query.

**The single most important action for this week:** Add Organization schema to the homepage with founder, employee count, and Hudson address to establish entity recognition in Google's Knowledge Graph.

## The five deliverables in this audit

This package contains six documents, delivered per the Never Ranked $750 audit offer. Each is a standalone deliverable.

### 1. Technical Audit (`02-technical-audit.md`)
Core on-page hygiene audit covering canonical tags, title tags, meta descriptions, heading structure, OpenGraph tags, image alt text, and word count across the sampled homepage.

**Top finding:** 100% of sampled pages are missing canonical tags, which fragments link equity and creates indexing ambiguity.

### 2. Schema Review (`03-schema-review.md`)
Full JSON-LD structured data analysis covering Organization, WebSite, BreadcrumbList, FAQPage, SoftwareApplication, and AggregateRating schema types with pasteable code blocks.

**Top finding:** Zero structured data deployed anywhere on the site, which means Google and AI engines have no machine-readable profile for Emanate Wireless.

### 3. Keyword Gap Analysis (`04-keyword-gap.md`)
SERP analysis of 12 commercial and informational queries in the healthcare RTLS space, identifying where competitors rank and where Emanate Wireless is absent.

**Top finding:** Emanate Wireless does not rank in the top 50 results for any core buyer queries like "real-time location system healthcare" or "RTLS hospital asset tracking."

### 4. AI Citation Audit (`05-ai-citations.md`)
Live testing across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude for 8 RTLS queries to measure citation share and identify what structured signals competitors use to earn mentions.

**Top finding:** Zero citations across all tested queries and all four AI engines, while competitors with Organization schema and FAQPage markup earn 2 to 4 citations per query.

### 5. Competitor Teardown (`06-competitor-teardown.md`)
Technical and schema analysis of three direct RTLS competitors (CenTrak, Midmark RTLS, and Zebra Technologies MotionWorks) showing what structured data and content strategies they deploy.

**Top finding:** All three sampled competitors deploy Organization schema, and two deploy SoftwareApplication schema with feature lists and pricing signals that feed AI answer engines.

### 6. 90-Day Roadmap (`07-roadmap.md`)
Month-by-month action plan with task-level detail, effort estimates, and success metrics tracing back to every finding in the technical, schema, keyword, AI, and competitor sections.

**Top recommendation:** Deploy Organization and WebSite schema in month one to establish entity recognition before attempting content expansion or backlink outreach.

## Methodology

This audit was produced using the Never Ranked audit methodology:

1. **Intake:** Fetched the site, robots.txt, sitemap.xml. Analyzed URL structure.
2. **Sample selection:** Pulled 1 representative page (homepage) as the full site is currently single-page.
3. **Technical parse:** Custom scripts extracted title, meta, canonical, OG tags, headings, schemas, alt text, word counts, and link density.
4. **Schema parse:** Full JSON-LD block inspection with type extraction (found zero blocks).
5. **SERP testing:** Live searches across commercial, informational, and comparison queries in the healthcare RTLS space. Captured top 10 results and AI-synthesized summaries.
6. **Competitor fetch:** Raw HTML of 3 direct competitors (CenTrak, Midmark RTLS, Zebra MotionWorks). Same analysis applied.
7. **Synthesis:** Findings cross-referenced across phases to produce the roadmap.
