# Meeting opening — Mark Cunningham, American Savings Bank

Drafted 2026-05-20. ~2 minutes spoken. Memorized arc, not a script.

---

Mark, before I show you anything, I want to tell you what we don't do, because we used to claim it and we were wrong.

We sold a tool that puts code on your website and promised it gets you cited by ChatGPT and Perplexity and Google's AI answers. We built it. We ran a real test on ourselves. It didn't work. The LLM crawlers don't even execute the code. So we stopped selling it.

What actually works, and what we actually do now, is measurement across two layers. Five engines that search the live web and cite their sources. Two engines that answer purely from model knowledge, meaning what AI says about you when it cannot look anything up. We watch all seven every day. We track which queries about Hawaii banking they answer, who they cite, and where ASB shows up or doesn't. When someone asks ChatGPT "best bank in Honolulu for a small business loan" or Perplexity "who do I bank with in Hawaii if I just moved here," we know the answer the AI gives, we know who it cites, and we know whether you're in the conversation or your competitors are.

There are tools that already do a version of this. Profound, Peec, a handful of others. They give a brand a dashboard with a score and a list of mentions. That works if you have a marketing analyst whose job is to log in every day and interpret it. We hand you a forensic readout instead. Which exact queries, which exact engines, which exact competitors are being cited instead of you, and which kinds of sources the AI is pulling from. It reads like a research memo, not a dashboard.

We don't touch your systems. No code on your property, no integration, no data flowing in from your side. We're observing public AI engines from the outside. This isn't a security review project for your team. It's a research engagement.

What I'd like to do in the next half hour is show you what we already see for ASB across those seven engines, name where the gap is, and hand your team a prepped punch list of exactly what to change and in what order. We don't make the changes ourselves. Your team or your agency does the work. We keep score and we tell you where to aim.
