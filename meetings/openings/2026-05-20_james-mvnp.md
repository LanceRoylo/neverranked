# Meeting opening — James Malin, MVNP

Drafted 2026-05-20. ~2 minutes spoken. Memorized arc, not a script.

Context: friend-of-friend intro, not an agency-evaluating-vendor pitch. MVNP does not handle ASB's SEO or AEO. This is a coffee. Walk in curious. Leave with either a partnership shape or a referral, not a contract.

---

James, thanks for making time. Before we get into anything I want to be honest about what brought me here, because the company I would have pitched you six months ago is not the company I'm sitting in front of you with today.

The original product was a snippet you drop on a client site that's supposed to make ChatGPT and Perplexity and Google's AI answers cite them. We built it, we sold it, we had customers paying for it. Then we ran a real pre-registered test on ourselves and the result was zero. The LLM crawlers don't execute JavaScript. The snippet was structurally invisible to them. So we stopped selling it and rebuilt the company around what does work.

What does work is the measurement layer, and it has two parts. Five engines that search the live web and cite their sources. Two that answer purely from model knowledge. We watch all seven every day, in every category we run. We capture which queries get answered, who gets cited, and which kinds of sources the AI pulls from. The output is a forensic readout. Per query, per engine, per competitor, per source type. Not a dashboard you log into. A research memo you read.

The category already has tools. Profound and Peec are the well-funded ones. Both of them sell a dashboard to the brand directly and quietly imply they'll help with the optimization side too. We go the other way on purpose. We don't compete with the agency for the optimization spend. We don't touch the work. What we do is diagnose the AI visibility gap and hand your team a prepped punch list. Which queries, which engines, which competitors are eating your client's lunch, which kinds of sources the AI is pulling from, and what to change in what order. Your team executes and owns the client relationship. We're a force multiplier for your senior strategists, not a competitor for their hours. And because we run readouts across categories, the priorities we hand your team are informed by source-type patterns we have observed in similar verticals. A single-engine self-dashboard structurally cannot do that. Only the cross-category view can.

I'm not here to pitch you a contract today. A mutual friend thought we should know each other and I think he's right. What I'd like to do is show you a real forensic readout on a category you'd recognize, walk you through what the deliverable to your team actually looks like, and let you tell me whether something like this is useful to MVNP or whether the shape is wrong. I'd rather hear it now than build the wrong thing for six months.
