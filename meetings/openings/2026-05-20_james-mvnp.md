James, thanks for making time. A mutual friend thought we should know each other, and I think he's right, so I want to make this useful rather than a pitch.

Here is the problem I would want an agency like MVNP to care about. When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity for the best business in a category, the AI answers with two or three named businesses. For most of your clients' categories, you have no reliable way to know whether your client is one of those names, who is cited instead, or what the AI pulled from to decide. That is a blind spot, and it widens every quarter as more buying decisions start inside an AI answer instead of a Google results page.

What NeverRanked does is measure exactly that. Seven AI surfaces, every day. Five that search the live web and cite their sources, two that answer from model knowledge alone. We capture which queries get answered, who gets cited, and which kinds of sources the AI pulls from. The output is a forensic readout. Per query, per engine, per competitor, per source type. Not a dashboard you log into. A research memo your team reads.

Here is why it fits an agency specifically. The category's other tools, Profound and Peec, sell a dashboard to the brand directly and quietly imply they will help with the optimization side too. We go the other way on purpose. We do not compete with the agency for the optimization spend. We do not touch the work. We diagnose the AI visibility gap and hand your team a prepped punch list. Which queries, which competitors are eating your client's lunch, which sources the AI trusts, and what to change in what order. Your team executes and owns the client relationship. We are a force multiplier for your senior strategists, not a competitor for their hours. And because we run readouts across categories, the priorities we hand your team are informed by source-type patterns we have seen in similar verticals. A single-engine self-dashboard structurally cannot do that.

I am not here to pitch a contract. A mutual friend thought we should know each other and I think he is right. What I would like to do is show you a real forensic readout on a category you would recognize, walk you through what the deliverable to your team actually looks like, and have you tell me whether something like this is useful to MVNP or whether the shape is wrong. I would rather hear that now than build the wrong thing for six months.
