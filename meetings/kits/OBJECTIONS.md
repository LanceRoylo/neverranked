## ON PRICING

### "$4,500 for one category seems steep for a report"

> The number is what it is because of what is in the kickoff.
> Three weeks of daily measurement across seven AI surfaces.
> A competitive cohort across five or more named competitors.
> Source-type analysis across the full classifier (YouTube,
> Reddit, Wikipedia, forum, social, review directories,
> publications, owned, competitor). A research memo written for
> a decision-maker, not a marketing analyst. A prepped punch
> list ordered by impact with the technical specifics your team
> needs to execute. The closest analog to the deliverable in
> cost-of-comparable-work terms is a Forrester research
> engagement, which lists at five times that number for less
> custom output.

### "Can I get a smaller version?"

> Yes. A five-query pilot for one category at no cost. The
> output looks like the kickoff deliverable at about
> one-fifteenth the depth. The point is to let you see the
> shape, not to substitute for the full engagement. If you want
> a smaller paid version of the kickoff itself, the answer is
> no. The shape collapses below the current scope.

### "Why per category, not per business?"

> Because the unit of measurement is the question shape, not
> the brand. A Hawaii bank that wants to be cited for consumer
> banking queries and small-business banking queries is two
> different cohort studies. The competitors are different. The
> source types AI engines cite are different. The customer
> intent shapes are different. Pricing per category forces the
> scope to be honest. Otherwise we end up promising more than
> any single measurement engagement can produce.

### "Profound charges less"

> They might. Their product is different. Profound sells a
> dashboard you log into and see your mention count. That is a
> useful product if you have a marketing analyst whose job is
> to interpret the dashboard. We sell a research memo plus a
> prepped punch list. The bigger price gap, honestly, is the
> work-substitution: a memo your team can act on saves a senior
> analyst three to five hours a month. Whether that math works
> for you depends on what your senior analysts cost.

## ON THE BOUNDARY

### "I want you to just do the work for me"

> I appreciate that. The reason we will not is not modesty. It
> is that vendors who promise measurement AND execution end up
> doing neither well, and the buyer never knows which lever
> produced the result. We went the other way on purpose. Your
> team or your agency does the execution. We do the
> measurement and the prepped guidance. If you do not have an
> implementation team for the work the readout surfaces, that
> is a fair concern and I can name three or four agencies in
> Hawaii that ship this kind of work cleanly.

### "Can you at least write the schema for us?"

> No. Not because it is hard. Because it is the wrong tool to
> turn for AI citation behavior, and we have proven it with our
> own data. The retracted product was a schema-injection tool.
> What actually moves the needle is the upstream signals: third-
> party profile freshness, authority backlinks, named-entity
> presence on review platforms AI engines actually trust.
> Those are not schema deploys. They are work your team or your
> agency does on the surfaces themselves.

### "What if I do not have an agency or a team?"

> Then I would not recommend starting with our engagement. The
> punch list assumes someone executes against it. If you have
> nobody who can do the implementation work, the readout is
> shelf-art. The honest path is to either staff a marketing
> implementer first, retain an agency that handles AEO-shaped
> work, or wait. We can recommend a couple of Hawaii-local
> agencies if that helps.

## ON OUTCOMES AND ROI

### "What citation lift can I expect?"

> Zero promised. We measure. We do not predict. The thing that
> moves AI citation share is the work your team does after the
> readout, plus the underlying ecosystem (which we cannot
> control and which shifts on the AI engines' own training
> cycles). What we can tell you with confidence is where you
> stand today, across all seven engines, on the queries that
> matter to you. The lift question is unanswerable in advance
> and any vendor who answers it confidently is selling theater.

### "What is the measurable ROI?"

> The measurable thing is citation share over time on the
> queries you and your team identified as commercially
> important. We give you that number daily, with a delta memo
> monthly. Whether the cost of the engagement plus the cost of
> your team's implementation time is worth the citation share
> movement you see is a calculation only you can do, because
> only you know what a citation on each query is worth to your
> business. Some of our customers will not find the math works.
> That is honest information for them too.

### "How long until I see results?"

> The first daily measurement starts the day the queries are
> locked. The first delivered memo lands three weeks later.
> Whether citation share moves on the engagement is a function
> of what your team does after the punch list. If they execute
> none of the punch list, citation share probably does not
> move. If they execute the high-impact items, we see movement
> in our weekly tracking within four to eight weeks for most
> categories. AI engines retrain on their own cycles, so any
> claim more precise than that is invented.

## ON METHODOLOGY

### "How do you actually measure across these engines?"

> Daily API calls to each engine on a frozen query set you
> approve. Perplexity, ChatGPT search, Gemini grounded,
> Microsoft Copilot via Bing, Google AI Overviews — these five
> search the live web and return cited sources, which we
> capture and classify. Claude and Gemma answer purely from
> training data, no web fetch, which is the model-knowledge
> layer (what AI says about you when it cannot search). Each
> query runs three times per day for noise control. The raw
> data lives in your engagement's data store, exportable any
> time. The classifier source code is public.

### "How do I know your numbers are accurate?"

> Three things. The raw data is captured and stored, you can
> see the actual API responses we collected. The classifier
> source code is public, you can audit how a URL gets bucketed.
> Gemma is one of the seven engines specifically because it is
> open-weight; your compliance team can re-run the exact same
> prompts against Gemma and verify our numbers independently.
> If you find the numbers wrong, we want to know.

### "How does this scale across our portfolio?"

> Per category. If you operate in five distinct categories,
> that is five kickoffs and five retainers, scoped per category.
> The aggregate intelligence compounds as we run more
> categories for you. By the third or fourth category, the
> source-type and engine-pattern observations we hand you on
> the new category are informed by your own data on the prior
> ones. That is real leverage and it cannot be matched by a
> dashboard product that does not know about the other four
> categories.

## ON AGENCY DYNAMICS

### "I already have an agency"

> Good. They do the execution. We add the diagnostic and
> measurement layer on top. The deliverable goes to your team
> AND to your agency, depending on how you scope the
> engagement. The punch list is built so a senior agency
> strategist can repackage it as their own recommendation to
> you, in their voice, and ship the work. We are a force
> multiplier for your agency's senior time, not a replacement
> for them.

### "Will you talk to my agency directly?"

> Yes, with your sign-off. Most engagements are scoped so the
> readout goes to whoever you designate, including your
> agency's account lead. We are not trying to insert ourselves
> between you and the agency. The relationship stays yours.

### "If my agency could do this themselves, why pay you?"

> Most agencies cannot. Not because they lack skill, but
> because the cross-engine measurement requires sustained
> infrastructure investment (API integrations across seven
> engines, daily scheduled runs, query-set discipline, the
> classifier, the aggregate dataset over time). Agencies bill
> their hours on client work, not on building a measurement
> dataset. The diagnostic layer is something agencies are
> structurally bad at producing in-house, and tend to be glad
> to receive when someone else does the upstream work.

### "What if I want to scope down or stop?"

> Kickoff is non-refundable once measurement work has begun.

### "Who owns the data after we stop?"

> You. Everything in the engagement (the raw measurement, the

> days then delete it. The category-level aggregate patterns

> engagement value. Everything else is usually fine. If your
> security team has questions, the answer to most of them is
> "we do not touch your systems" — see EMAIL-TEMPLATES.md

## ON URGENCY AND TIMING

### "Why now, not next quarter?"

> Two honest reasons. One: AI citation patterns shift every
> time the engines retrain. The longer you wait, the more
> baseline drift there is and the harder it gets to attribute
> any future change in citation share to your team's work
> versus the underlying ecosystem moving. Starting now gives
> you a longer time series to actually understand the movement.
> Two: the cost of starting is fixed ($4,500 + $1,500/mo), and
> the cost of waiting is the citation share you do not earn in
> the queries your buyers are asking today. The math has been
> done.

### "Is there a discount for paying annually?"

> No. The pricing is what it is. If you want to pre-pay 12
> months, we will cash the invoice and the engagement runs as
> normal. The reason we do not discount for annual is that the
> price is anchored on the value of the work, not on financing.
