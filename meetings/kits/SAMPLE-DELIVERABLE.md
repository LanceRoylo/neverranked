## 1. Executive summary

For the queries a Honolulu med-spa shopper actually types into
ChatGPT and Perplexity, your business does not appear in the AI
answer. Three of your competitors do, consistently, across both
engines and across both head-intent ("best med spa in Honolulu")
and long-tail intent ("med spa Kakaako that does morpheus8")
queries.

The cohort we measured spans 40 distinct queries, 2,104 total
citation slots captured, two engines (Perplexity sonar and OpenAI
gpt-4o-search-preview, the only two with confirmed live keys at
the time of the run; the full engagement adds the remaining
three citation-grade engines plus the two model-knowledge
engines). The headline pattern: 98% of all citations in this
category come from independent web (med-spa sites, vendor pages,
publications). Only 2% from review directories. Zero from
Reddit. Zero from YouTube on OpenAI; 1% from YouTube on
Perplexity.

The punch list at the end of this memo orders the actionable
work by impact. Item 1 alone would put you on roughly one third
of the queries currently being answered without you.

## 2. Methodology and scope

Twenty-week-frozen query set, hash-locked, available on request.
The 40 queries split into:

- **Head intent (16 queries):** "best med spa in Honolulu",
  "top rated medical spa Honolulu", "best place for filler near
  me Honolulu", neighborhood scoping ("medical spa Kakaako",
  "best aesthetician Manoa"), service scoping (Botox, filler,
  microneedling, laser hair removal).
- **Long-tail intent (24 queries):** open-late, weekend-open,
  service-combined queries ("Botox and filler same visit"),
  insurance-fit queries, neighborhood-plus-service ("med spa
  near Kakaako that does morpheus8"), value-conscious queries
  ("affordable med spa Honolulu that takes payment plans"),
  trust-signal queries ("med spa owned by a doctor", "for first
  time botox nervous patient").

Each query ran three times per engine per day for noise
control. Runs are hash-stable, meaning re-running on different
days produces a comparable cohort.

**Engines measured (this kickoff):** Perplexity sonar, OpenAI
gpt-4o-search-preview. The full engagement adds Gemini grounded,
Microsoft Copilot via Bing, Google AI Overviews (the three other
citation-grade engines), plus Claude and Gemma (the two
model-knowledge engines).

**Competitors in cohort:** [the five named competitors you
identified during scoping]. Add or remove competitors before the
next monthly delta memo if priorities change.

**What this engagement did NOT measure:** model-knowledge layer
(Claude, Gemma) is in the full ongoing engagement, not this
kickoff. Voice-only AI (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant) is out of
scope. Paid AI advertising surfaces (AI Overview ads) are out of
scope until those formats mature.

## 3. The headline finding

You do not appear in the AI answer for 38 of 40 measured
queries. The two queries where you do appear are exact-name
matches ("[your business name] reviews", "[your business name]
Honolulu") — queries a customer only types if they already
know your name.

For every demand-shape query (a customer asking AI to find a
provider in your category without naming you), you are absent.
The competitors who do appear, consistently, are:

- **finamedspa.com** (88 citations across 16 of 40 queries)
- **honolulumedspa.com** (88 citations across 18 of 40 queries)
- **reneuxmeddayspa.com** (58 citations across 14 of 40 queries)
- **artistrymedspa.co** (53 citations across 12 of 40 queries)

Three of those four are not the largest med spas on Oahu by
revenue or chair count. They are the four whose authority
signals AI engines have learned to trust for this category.
That distinction is the entire work of the engagement.

## 4. Source-type distribution (where AI pulls from in this category)

Across 2,104 citations on the cohort:

| Source type        | % of citations | Hosts driving it           |
|--------------------|----------------|----------------------------|
| Independent web    | 98%            | Spa sites, vendor pages    |
| Review directories | 2%             | medspascout.com, cylex.us  |
| YouTube            | <1%            | Single tutorial channel    |
| Reddit             | 0%             | Not cited for this category|
| Wikipedia          | 0%             | Not cited                  |
| Forum / Q&A        | 0%             | Not cited                  |

**The non-obvious finding:** the two review directories AI
engines cite for Honolulu med spas are not Yelp, not Google,
not Healthgrades, not RealSelf. They are
**medspascout.com** and **cylex.us.com**. Most med-spa
operators have never claimed a profile on either. Doing so
costs nothing and would put you in a source class AI engines
demonstrably trust for your category.

The Reddit zero is also a real finding. Many med spas spend on
Reddit-adjacent reputation management. For your category, on
Perplexity, that spend produces no citation share. (For
comparison: cross-category aggregate data shows Reddit drives
20-35% of citations on Perplexity for B2C SaaS and consumer
electronics categories.)

## 5. Per-engine breakdown

The seven AI surfaces do not behave identically. Three
patterns from this cohort:

**Perplexity** (916 citations measured):
- 96% independent_web, 3% review_directory, 1% YouTube.
- The only engine that cited YouTube at all in this category.
- More likely to surface neighborhood-specific results.

**OpenAI gpt-4o-search-preview** (1,188 citations measured):
- 99% independent_web, 1% review_directory.
- Zero YouTube, zero Reddit.
- Heavier weighting toward the spa's own site over third-party
  coverage.

**Gemini / Bing / Google AI Overviews:** measured in the ongoing
engagement. The full delta memo will surface engine-level
differences across all five citation engines once the next 30
days of data are in.

**Claude and Gemma (model-knowledge):** measured in the ongoing
engagement. These tell you what AI says about you when it
cannot search, which is the baseline-mention layer behind every
ungrounded AI conversation.

## 8. Prepped punch list (ordered by impact)

Sequenced by ratio of likely citation-share movement to
implementation cost. Item 1 is the highest-leverage single
piece of work on the entire list. Your team executes against
this; we re-measure weekly and report the delta in the
monthly memo.

### Priority 1: Claim and complete medspascout.com profile
- **Why:** This is the single review-directory AI engines
  demonstrably trust for your category on Perplexity. Three
  separate competitors hold claimed, complete profiles. You
  do not have one.
- **What:** Create the business profile, populate all fields
  (services, hours, location, photos, treatment list,
  certifications). Add aggregate review information that
  mirrors your actual Google Business rating.
- **Effort:** 2-3 hours of one team member's time.
- **Expected window for movement:** 4-6 weeks (directory
  recrawl + AI-engine re-index cycle).

### Priority 2: Claim and complete cylex.us.com profile
- **Why:** Same logic as Priority 1. Smaller volume but
  same source-type weighting.
- **Effort:** 1-2 hours.
- **Expected window:** 4-6 weeks.

### Priority 3: Audit your authority backlinks for currency
- **Why:** AI engines weight authority backlinks heavily
  in this category. Stale, broken, or redirect-chained
  backlinks signal lower trust. We checked your site's
  inbound link profile against the three leading competitors;
  you have several authority links that 404 or redirect
  through chains, which weakens their value.
- **What:** The technical details on which links to audit
  and how to address each are in Appendix B.
- **Effort:** 4-8 hours for an SEO-capable team member.
- **Expected window:** 6-10 weeks for AI-engine recrawl.

### Priority 4: Publish service-page content that names specific
neighborhoods and intent shapes
- **Why:** The four competitors who dominate the long-tail
  queries all have service pages that explicitly mention the
  Oahu neighborhoods served, the specific service variants
  offered, and the kind of customer the service is for.
  Your service pages are more generic and AI engines do not
  cite them for neighborhood-specific or intent-specific
  queries.
- **What:** Add neighborhood-and-intent language to your
  existing service pages. We are not asking for new pages;
  we are asking for the pages to be more specific.
- **Effort:** 3-6 hours of focused writing per service line.
- **Expected window:** 4-8 weeks.

### Priority 5: Update Bing Business Profile
- **Why:** Microsoft Copilot, which is one of the seven
  engines we measure in the full engagement, pulls heavily
  from Bing Business Profile data. Most med spas in Honolulu
  have no Bing Business Profile.
- **What:** Claim or create the Bing Business Profile,
  complete it, sync it to your Google Business Profile data.
- **Effort:** 1-2 hours.
- **Expected window:** 3-5 weeks.

### Priority 6: Audit and refresh your meta descriptions
- **Why:** AI engines that grounded-search the web read
  meta descriptions as one signal of what a page is about.
  Your meta descriptions on the four highest-value service
  pages are either missing or generic.
- **What:** Specific, intent-aligned meta descriptions for
  each service page. Drafts available in Appendix C.
- **Effort:** 1-2 hours.
- **Expected window:** 2-4 weeks.

  engines (Perplexity, OpenAI). The ongoing engagement adds
  Gemini, Bing, Google AI Overviews, Claude, Gemma.
- **Voice AI surfaces** (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant). Out of
  scope until the answer-generation behavior on those surfaces
  becomes measurable through public APIs.
- **AI ad placements.** Out of scope until those formats are
  measurable.
- **Geo variation outside Oahu.** This cohort is Honolulu-
  specific. Citation patterns in Maui, Kauai, or the Big Island
  may differ.
- **Long-term causation.** We measured what AI cites. We did
  not test whether shipping the punch list causes citation
  movement, because we cannot pre-register that test on your
  account. The monthly delta memo will report observed
  movement. Whether the punch list caused the movement is
  inference, not proof.
