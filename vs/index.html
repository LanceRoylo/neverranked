NeverRanked vs Profound, Peec, AthenaHQ.

Structural differences, named honestly. Where the competition is doing something well, we say so. Where the difference is real, we name the specifics.

Reading this honestly: the right choice depends on what you actually want. If you want a self-serve dashboard you log into, Profound or Peec is the right answer and this page will tell you so. NeverRanked is for buyers who want a research memo, a fail-closed factual gate before anything ships, and a structural no-execution boundary. Those are different products. Pick the one that matches what you're solving for.

The categorical difference in one sentence

Profound and Peec sell a dashboard. You log into their interface, configure your prompts and competitors, and read the trends. The product is the surface you interact with.

NeverRanked sells a research engagement. Daily measurement happens in the background. The output is a monthly research memo plus a prepped punch list, delivered to your team. The product is the artifact and the analysis.

Both shapes are valid. The math of which fits your operation depends on whether you have someone whose job is to log into dashboards and interpret trends (Profound or Peec is right) or whether you'd rather have a memo land in your inbox once a month with the analysis already done (NeverRanked is right).

What each competitor actually does

Profound

Nine AI engines tracked (Perplexity, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, Microsoft Copilot, Meta AI, DeepSeek, Google AI Overviews). That is a wider engine surface than ours; we cover seven. Profound delivers a dashboard with "Answer Engine Insights" plus a separate free "AEO Report" offering. They have launched "Agents" — autonomous workers for marketing functions, which is a different product direction we will not follow. Funded company at meaningful scale.

What Profound is good for: a brand that wants the widest possible engine coverage and is comfortable consuming insights via a logged-in dashboard. Larger marketing teams with someone whose role includes regular dashboard review will get the most value.

Peec AI

Three AI engines tracked (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini). Narrower engine surface than ours. Peec does include competitor benchmarking — you add brands and the dashboard ranks you against them. Recommendations-only product; no execution by Peec. Dashboard plus CSV exports and Looker Studio integration for custom reporting.

What Peec is good for: a brand that primarily cares about ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini and wants competitor benchmarking in a dashboard surface they can export to BI tools. If your analytics team lives in Looker, this is the cleanest path.

AthenaHQ

Brand-mention monitoring across AI engines, similar product shape to Profound but with a tighter editorial focus on the citation-tracking dimension. Public information on exact engine coverage and pricing is limited.

What AthenaHQ is good for: a brand that wants to see the raw citation events as they happen and is willing to do its own analysis on top.

When to pick each one

Pick Profound if

  • You want the widest possible engine coverage today.
  • Your team has someone whose role includes regular dashboard review.
  • You're interested in their Agents direction (autonomous AI doing marketing functions).
  • You operate at a scale where a funded platform vendor matches your procurement comfort better than a small founder-led firm.

Pick Peec if

  • Your category is well-served by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini specifically.
  • Your analytics team lives in Looker Studio or similar BI tools and wants exports rather than memos.
  • You want competitor benchmarking via dashboard you configure yourself.

Pick AthenaHQ if

  • You want raw citation events as they happen and prefer to do analysis yourself.

Pick NeverRanked if

  • You'd rather have a research memo land in your inbox than log into a dashboard.
  • You want the fail-closed factual gate that prevents anything overclaimed from reaching your customers.
  • You want the pre-registration discipline that killed our prior product to be the same discipline applied to your engagement.
  • You want a vendor that will not try to execute the work and create attribution ambiguity.
  • You want position-in-answer and drift detection layered on top of the cross-engine cross-competitor citation tracking.
  • You're a research-oriented buyer who values an analyst-style deliverable over a self-serve product surface.
  • You value direct founder access over scaled platform vendor processes.
