## What MVNP needs to hear (in this order)

### 1. AEO is adjacent to paid digital, not in conflict with it

> "AEO is a structured-data and content infrastructure layer. It does not compete with what MVNP runs for ASB. Two clean paths: (a) MVNP becomes NR's agency partner and runs AEO as a new line item billable to ASB at MVNP's own rate, or (b) ASB engages NR directly, MVNP keeps doing paid digital, and the two work in parallel. We have a strong preference for (a)."

This kills the "are you trying to take our seat" worry in the first 60 seconds.

### 2. Wholesale economics, one slide

| Tier | NR wholesale to MVNP | Suggested MVNP retail to client | MVNP gross margin / slot / mo |

| Signal | $800/mo | $2,000/mo | **$1,200** |

Plus 3 to 8 billable implementation hours per slot per month, at MVNP's hourly rate. Implementation is yours if you want it; ours if you'd rather not staff it.

### 4. Founding-partner terms (Hawaii vertical exclusivity)

The first Hawaii agency to sign at **3+ active Signal slots within 60 days** gets exclusive Hawaii rights to that client's vertical for 12 months, renewable. Lockout is per-vertical, so MVNP can choose where it wants air cover first. ASB = Hawaii community banking vertical locked.

The lockout means: if another Hawaii agency tries to pitch NeverRanked into a Hawaii community-banking client, we route it back through MVNP. Worth offering specifically to a founding partner — not available later.

### 5. Client-ownership pattern

MVNP holds the retainer. NR is a margin line. The schema deployment lives on the client's site, served by a snippet that can be disabled in 30 seconds. There is **no lock-in mechanism on the NR side** — if the relationship ends, we end with it. Your job is the client relationship; ours is the citation infrastructure underneath it.

## The "why MVNP, why now" positioning

Don't sell features. Sell the asymmetric opportunity:

> "AEO is going to be a line item on every Hawaii business's marketing budget within 18 months. Right now, zero Hawaii agencies are positioned to deliver it. Whichever agency moves first stakes a category. We have zero agency partners signed. Whichever Hawaii agency signs first gets vertical lockout terms we won't offer to agency #2. The opening to be 'the agency that owns AEO in Hawaii' is open for the next 30 to 60 days, not the next 6 months."

The argument is positioning, not pricing. If MVNP only hears the margins, they'll comparison-shop. If they hear the positioning, they ask "how do we move?"

## MVNP-specific objections drilled (beyond the 2 in `07-qa-prep.md`)

These supplement the existing Q&A; if James pushes on any of these mid-meeting, this is the canonical answer.

### "Does NR ever sell direct to a Hawaii client?"

> "We sell direct today because we have no agency partner today. The moment MVNP signs founding terms, we route every inbound Hawaii community-banking lead through MVNP. We're explicit about this: founding partner = NR's outbound effort in that vertical becomes MVNP's pipeline. We'd rather grow one agency channel than build a competing direct sales motion."

### "Will ASB see the wholesale price?"

> "No. ASB sees MVNP's retail price on MVNP's invoice. The NR contract is between NR and MVNP — ASB is not a party to it and has no reason to ask about wholesale. The only situation where wholesale becomes visible is if ASB asks MVNP directly for an unbundled invoice, which is a question about MVNP's pricing structure, not ours. Our standard partner agreement includes a 'no disclosure of wholesale to client' clause if you want it."

### "Who does the implementation work — your team or ours?"

> "Either. The Signal tier ships our snippet that does ~80% of the schema work autonomously; MVNP's team needs ~3 hours per month per client to handle the parts that touch the client's CMS and approve content before it publishes. The Amplify tier is the same plus we generate per-client content; you still review and approve. If MVNP would rather we handle implementation directly, we white-label that for $400/slot/mo on top of wholesale. Most agencies bill the 3-8 hours directly to the client and keep that margin too."

### "How do you handle content quality? Who's the final editor?"

> "Every piece of generated content runs through a structured grader before it ships — voice match, fact-check against the client's profile, no marketing-tells (no em dashes, no formulaic openers, no AI-sounding copy). MVNP gets a review queue in the dashboard. Nothing publishes to the client's domain without explicit MVNP approval on each artifact. We can show that queue in the live demo."

### "What if a client wants to leave you mid-pilot?"

(From `01-GAMEPLAN.md` Objection 16, kept here for parity)

> "MVNP owns the contract. The schema deployment is on the client's site, served by a snippet you can disable in 30 seconds. The data and the FAQ schema are theirs to keep. No lock-in mechanism on the NR side — the work is structured to be reproducible by anyone with the same tools. Your job is the relationship; if the relationship ends, we end with it."

### "Why isn't another Hawaii agency already doing this?"

> "Same reason no Hawaii agency offered programmatic SEO in 2014 or paid-social-with-attribution in 2019 — the tooling didn't exist, the buyer didn't know to ask, and the agencies were focused on the channels they already sold. The window opens when one operator builds the infrastructure and one agency commits to selling it. That's this conversation."

## What to AVOID saying to MVNP

Pulled from `01-GAMEPLAN.md` line 137 and expanded:

1. **Don't pitch the Reddit reply brief feature.** Deprecated. If MVNP brings it up because they read the prior pitch material, redirect: "We pivoted to FAQ deployment because it puts the work on us and leaves the client controlling their own domain. Better for compliance, better for the client."
2. **Don't promise client volume.** "Three more MVNP clients in 90 days" is an aspiration we share, not a commitment we make.
3. **Don't undersell ASB's bargaining position.** Mark is in the room. If MVNP-side language sounds like "ASB is just our first slot," Mark hears himself reduced to a slot. He isn't.
4. **Don't frame this as MVNP needing NR.** Frame it as "we both think this category opens in 2026, and we'd like to open it together in Hawaii." Symmetric.
5. **Don't quote a price faster than asked.** Wholesale pricing belongs in the partner conversation, not the buyer conversation. If ASB is in the room when wholesale comes up, redirect: "Wholesale is a separate conversation MVNP and I would have after this meeting."
