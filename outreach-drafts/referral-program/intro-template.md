# Warm intro templates — for Katy to adapt

Use whichever fits. Keep it short. The goal is a yes to "want me to connect you," then a clean handoff. Don't oversell, don't explain the product, don't make claims.

---

## Generic (any category)

Hi [Name],

I want to connect you with Lance Roylo, who runs a company called NeverRanked. They measure how AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google's AI answers) recommend businesses in a given space, and help businesses get named when buyers ask. With more and more people finding [your category] through AI now, I thought it was worth a conversation.

Lance, meet [Name], [title] at [company]. I'll let you two take it from here.

[Katy]

## Ruth Law Team / getjustice.com specific

Hi [Name],

He runs NeverRanked, which measures how AI search tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity recommend law firms when people ask things like "best personal injury lawyer in Tampa," and helps firms show up in those answers. Given how much of the search has moved that direction, I thought it was a conversation worth having for the firm.

Lance, meet [Name] at the Ruth Law Team. I'll step out and let you two connect.

[Katy]

## Notes for Katy

- Send it to the decision-maker (managing partner, owner, head of marketing), not a gatekeeper if you can help it.
- "I'll let you two take it from here" is the whole move. After the intro, you're done. Lance handles everything.
- If they ask you questions about it before the intro, the honest answer is: "I only know it at a high level, that's why I want to connect you with Lance directly." That keeps you from accidentally over-promising.
