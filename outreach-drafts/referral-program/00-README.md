## The model in one line

Referrer makes a warm introduction and steps away. Lance owns the pitch, the price, the delivery, and the customer. Referrer never sells or makes claims. This protects the brand (rigor is the moat) and keeps the referrer hands-off, which is what they want.

## The fee

Per introduced prospect that becomes a paying customer, per category:

- **20% of the kickoff** = $900 one-time (on the $4,500 kickoff), paid after the customer's kickoff payment clears
- **10% of the monthly** = $150/mo (on the $1,500/mo), paid monthly for as long as the customer stays active

Single-category deal: $900 + $150/mo. Year one = $2,700, then $1,800/year ongoing.
Multi-market client (e.g. Ruth Law across FL/GA/MN as 3 categories): ~$2,700 in kickoff cuts + $450/mo = ~$8,100 year one.

Affordable because margins are ~85-95% (fixed cost ~$220/mo total) and distribution is the constraint. Dial the percentages if you want, but keep them generous for the first referrers.

## The gate — do NOT formally proceed until all three are true

1. [ ] **LLC formed** — then drop the entity name into `introducer-agreement.md`
2. [ ] **Insurance bound** — Tech E&O + Cyber + Media Liability (Media is the critical line for competitive-claims work)
3. [ ] **Agreement signed** — Katy agrees to `introducer-agreement.md`

Once 1-3 are green, the referrer package is live and Katy can make the warm intro. Until then: the write-up can be prepped, but no pitch with competitive claims goes out.

## One note on the agreement

It's a plain founder-to-friend starting template, written to be readable, not lawyered. Once the LLC exists and before Katy signs, run it past a lawyer for a quick glance. A simple written arrangement is the right starting point; the lawyer pass is cheap insurance on the language.
