What is AEO?

It stands for Answer Engine Optimization. Think of it as the new version of SEO. SEO was about ranking in Google's list of blue links. AEO is about getting named when an AI answers a question directly.

When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity or Google's new AI answers "who's the best personal injury lawyer in Tampa," they don't get a page of ten links anymore. They get a short answer that names two or three specific firms. AEO is the work of being one of the firms it names.

How is that different from SEO?

SEO gives you a spot in a list, and the person scrolls and picks. AEO gives the person a recommendation with only a few names in it. If you are not one of those names, you do not exist for that question. There is no page two.

SEO is a shelf of options. AEO is the recommendation. Being left off the recommendation is a different kind of invisible.

There is one more difference that matters: with SEO you could always see your rankings. With AEO, almost nobody has been measuring who the AI actually names. That blind spot is the gap I work in.

Is this real, or is it hype?

Fair question, and the honest answer is that it is early and moving fast. But the direction is hard to argue with. Hundreds of millions of people use ChatGPT. Google now puts an AI answer above the links on a huge share of searches. More and more people ask AI for recommendations the way they used to type into Google.

Nobody can promise it stays exactly like this. What is clear is that the businesses getting named early have an edge, and the ones even measuring it are ahead of the ones who have no idea. That is a good place to be early.

Which AI tools are we talking about?

The major ones people actually use: ChatGPT, Google's AI Overviews, Perplexity, Microsoft Copilot, Google Gemini, and Claude. I measure across them, because a business can be named by one and invisible on the rest, and that gap is the whole story.

Can you even influence what the AI says? Isn't it a black box?

You cannot control it, but you can influence it, the same way you could always influence Google without controlling it. AI engines pull from the open web, from structured data on a site, from reviews, directories, and credible content. When a business is clearly set up and well represented for a topic, the AI is far more likely to name it.

It is not magic and it is not guaranteed. But it is not random either. I measure what is actually moving the needle in a given category, and hand the business a specific list of what to fix.

So what does NeverRanked actually do?

Two things. I measure, rigorously, across those AI tools, whether a business gets named for the real buying questions in its category, and which competitors get named instead. Then I diagnose: a research memo and a prioritized to-do list.

I do not do the implementation. The business's own team or their agency executes the list. That separation is on purpose. You cannot credibly measure something while also being the one paid to move the number. Keeping those apart is what makes the measurement trustworthy instead of a sales pitch about my own work.

Why would a business pay for this?

Because being the name AI gives when someone asks a high-value buying question is worth real money, especially in categories where a single customer is valuable. A law firm. A financial advisor. A specialty practice. And right now most of them have no idea whether AI names them or sends every one of those questions to a competitor.

For a business that already spends to get found, finding out you are invisible in the place buyers are increasingly looking is not a nice-to-know. It is urgent.
