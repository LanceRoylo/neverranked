NVI Score Methodology

The NeverRanked Visibility Index (NVI), also called the AI Presence Score, is a 0-100 measurement of how present a brand is across the AI engines that now answer most "best X for Y" queries. It is the headline metric in the monthly NVI report that NeverRanked publishes for tracked clients.

The NVI is distinct from the AEO Score. The AEO Score measures whether a site has the structured-data signals AI engines need (schema, llms.txt, technical hygiene). The NVI measures whether the engines are actually citing the brand, how often, in what position, and with what tone. AEO is the input. NVI is the outcome.

The formula

NVI is a weighted sum of four sub-signals. The four weights add to 100. There are no hidden multipliers, no proprietary boost factors, no "secret sauce." The arithmetic is below.

NVI = citation_rate_pts (40)
    + engine_spread_pts  (25)
    + prominence_pts     (20)
    + sentiment_pts      (15)

Each component is described in its own section. The full implementation is open source at dashboard/src/nvi/score.ts in the public repo. Anyone can read the function, run it against the same input rows, and reproduce our number.

1. Citation rate (40 points)

The percentage of tracked prompts where the brand appears in any engine's answer. A prompt is one of the 14-19 industry-specific questions in the brand's tracked keyword set ("best business checking account in Hawaii," "top performing arts venues in Honolulu," etc.). The set is fixed at the start of each reporting period so the denominator is stable.

citation_rate_pts = (prompts_cited / prompts_total) * 40

Worked example: 14 of 19 prompts cited the brand at least once across the engine panel. citation_rate_pts = 14/19 * 40 = 29.47.

2. Engine spread (25 points)

How many distinct engines cited the brand at least once. NVI tracks five engines for the spread bonus: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews, and Microsoft Copilot. Claude is observed but excluded from this component because Claude responses are training-only (no live web citation surface). Gemma is observed for reproducibility but uses a separate weighting block on the NVI report.

engine_spread_pts = min(25, engines_cited * (25 / 5))

Worked example: 4 of 5 tracked engines cited the brand at least once. engine_spread_pts = 4 * 5 = 20.

3. Prominence (20 points)

Where the brand appeared in each engine's answer. Prominence 1 is the first/hero mention. Prominence 10 is the last/footnote-tier mention. Each cited row maps to a per-row score of (11 - p) / 10, so rank 1 = 1.0 and rank 10 = 0.1. The per-row scores are averaged across all cited rows that carry a prominence value, then scaled by the 20-point cap.

per_row = (11 - prominence) / 10        # 1.0 (best) to 0.1 (worst)
prominence_pts = average(per_row across cited rows) * 20

Worked example: brand was cited 12 times across the run. Prominence values: [1, 2, 2, 3, 1, 4, 5, 6, 2, 3, 4, 7]. Average per-row score = (1.0 + 0.9 + 0.9 + 0.8 + 1.0 + 0.7 + 0.6 + 0.5 + 0.9 + 0.8 + 0.7 + 0.4) / 12 = 0.767. prominence_pts = 0.767 * 20 = 15.33.

Rows without a prominence value (legacy data before migration 0058) are excluded from the denominator so historical reports are not penalized for missing the column.

4. Sentiment (15 points)

Net positive minus negative share of cited rows that have been sentiment-scored. Each cited row is classified positive, neutral, or negative by a separate scoring pass (see sentiment-scorer.ts). Neutral rows and unscored rows contribute zero to the numerator.

scored_rows = cited rows where sentiment is not null
net = (positive_count - negative_count) / scored_rows.length
sentiment_pts = max(0, min(15, net * 15))

Worked example: 10 of 12 cited rows are sentiment-scored. 7 positive, 2 neutral, 1 negative. net = (7 - 1) / 10 = 0.6. sentiment_pts = 0.6 * 15 = 9.0.

How the data is collected

Every weekday at 06:00 UTC NeverRanked dispatches one query per tracked keyword to each engine via the engine's official API or public search surface. Each response is parsed for brand citations, the brand's position in the answer, and the sentiment of any descriptive text around the citation. Raw rows land in the citation_runs D1 table. The NVI for a reporting period is computed from those rows at month-end.

Engine list and how each is reached:

  • ChatGPT: OpenAI API with web search enabled
  • Perplexity: Perplexity Sonar API
  • Claude: Anthropic API (training-only, excluded from spread)
  • Gemini: Google Gemini API with grounding
  • Microsoft Copilot: Bing-grounded chat API
  • Google AI Overviews: SERP synthesis via DataForSEO
  • Gemma: Together AI inference on the open-weight Gemma model, which lets anyone re-run our prompts on the same weights and verify our numbers without trusting us

What changes an NVI

  • Schema deployments (the AEO Score work): typically move NVI 5-15 points within 30 days as engines re-crawl and start citing the brand.
  • Content publishing in the brand's voice on the tracked topics: moves NVI through both citation_rate (more queries answered with the brand) and prominence (brand becomes the lead source on more queries).
  • Reddit and authority-platform presence (G2, Trustpilot, Capterra, Google Business): moves prominence and sentiment because engines weight these as trust signals.
  • Press mentions and direct media coverage: moves citation_rate and engine_spread.

What does NOT change an NVI

  • Backlink quantity, Domain Authority, page rank. AI engines weight schema + citation patterns over link graph for source selection.
  • Paid placements. Paid links and sponsored content do not enter the engines' answer-generation paths in any way we have observed.
  • Site speed beyond basic loadability. A slow site is still cited if its content is right.
  • The brand's own marketing claims about itself. Engines weight third-party mentions and structured data, not first-person self-description.

Independence and reproducibility

Six of the seven tracked engines are commercial APIs. We cannot audit what they do behind the scenes; we have to trust their responses. The seventh, Gemma, is Google's open-weight model. Its weights are public. Anyone with the same model weights can re-run our prompts and verify the citation numbers we observe. We track Gemma specifically because it lets us anchor the methodology to a reproducible baseline.

The full scoring function, the engine integration code, and the data schema are open source at github.com/LanceRoylo/neverranked.

Errata and disputes

If a client disputes a score, we re-run the engine panel within 24 hours and publish the second run alongside the first. NVI scores that move more than 8 points between consecutive runs get manual review and either a corrected number or a "review pending" flag on the next report.

What the NVI does NOT claim

The NVI does not predict revenue, conversion, brand value, customer satisfaction, or financial fitness. It is a measurement of one specific thing: whether AI engines are citing the brand across a fixed prompt set with appropriate position and tone. Sites with low NVIs often have excellent businesses underneath; the score measures the citation layer, not the business.

The NVI also does not measure direct traffic. AI engines that cite a brand do not always send a click. Citation share is a leading indicator of future direct-search interest, not a measure of current click volume.

Versioning

The current methodology is v2 (this document). v1 (deprecated, 2025) used 50/30/20 weights without the prominence component. v2 (current, 2026) rebalanced to 40/25/20/15 once the prominence and sentiment columns were populated across the row history. Methodology changes are announced in the NeverRanked changelog and re-applied retroactively to historical reports so year-over-year comparisons remain meaningful.
