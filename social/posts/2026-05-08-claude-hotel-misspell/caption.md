I asked Claude "best boutique hotel in Honolulu" this morning.

It recommended Halekulani. A $1,000-a-night iconic Hawaii hotel

Then it spelled the name wrong.

"Haalekulani." Not Halekulani.

Read that twice. The world's most-used AI engine, asked for the most

Why? Halekulani's website doesn't expose the structured data the
AI would read first. So Claude falls back to the messier sources.
Travel blogs. OTAs. Wikipedia mirror sites. Some of them spell
it wrong. The wrong spelling propagates. Claude lifts it.

Now extend the thought to your business. ChatGPT and Claude are
already telling buyers about you. The descriptions are coming from
sources you don't control.

Run this for your category. Open ChatGPT or Claude. Ask:

"best [your category] in [your city]"

Read the answer. Read how it describes you. That description is
your storefront for the next twelve months.

What does it say?
