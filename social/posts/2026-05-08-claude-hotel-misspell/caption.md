# LinkedIn Personal — AI is writing your business description right now

## What this is

Single-image post for Lance's personal LinkedIn. Reproducible scroll-stop:
the structural truth that AI engines describe businesses using
third-party sources, not the business's own site, because almost no
business has the schema deployed that would make their own framing
extractable. Image at `card.png`, 1080x1080.

## Caption (paste verbatim)

I asked Claude for the best boutique hotel in Honolulu this morning.

It returned five hotels with descriptions like "mid-century modern with a retro-cool aesthetic" and "elegant tranquil luxury."

Then I went to those hotels' own websites.

Not one of those phrases is on the hotels' own pages. Nowhere.

The descriptions came from Wikipedia, travel blogs, and OTA review aggregators. The hotels aren't writing their own AI answers. Travel bloggers and Yelp reviewers are.

Now run this for your own business.

Open ChatGPT or Claude. Ask "best [your category] in [your city]." Read how the engine describes you. Then go to your homepage and search for those exact phrases. You won't find them. The AI is using whatever a 2019 review or Wikipedia mirror had to say.

That description is your storefront for buyers who never click through. It is what they see, what they trust, and what they decide on. And it isn't yours.

What is the AI saying about your business right now?

## Why this version works

- **Reproducible.** Any reader who runs the prompt against their
  own category will see the same structural pattern: AI describes
  the business using third-party language. No specific data point
  that can fall apart between sessions.
- **Universal.** The hotel example is the proof, but the truth
  applies to every business in every category. Hotelier, lawyer,
  CPA, dentist, agency owner — they all see the same gap when they
  test it.
- **The lever (specificity) survives the pivot.** Reader can verify
  in 60 seconds: open AI, run prompt, check own website. The thing
  they verify is structural ("none of these phrases are on my
  site"), not anecdotal ("did the AI misspell my name today").
