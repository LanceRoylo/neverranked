## Why it works

Three things layered:

### 1. Tension Hold lever, surfaced in the image

The largest visual element is the buyer's wound, framed as a
question. The image does not pitch the product. It does not show
the product. It surfaces a regret the buyer has already had.
You have to read the body copy to resolve the tension. That is
what Tension Hold does — earn the second second of attention
before resolving the first.

Most AI-tool ads lead with the feature ("AI agents that do X").
This leads with the buyer's existing scar.

### 2. Specificity at every level

- **"$6K"** not "expensive"
- **"Wordpress"** not "your website"
- **"no leads"** not "underperforming"
- **Question, not statement** — invites self-identification

If they had written "Spent on a website that's not converting?"
the ad disappears into the feed. The named amount, named tool,
named outcome is what makes it stick.

### 3. Refusal of category aesthetic

No product screenshot. No abstract gradient. No AI orb. No
stock photography of someone smiling at a laptop. Just black
field plus editorial serif. Confident enough to be one
sentence. Reads like a New Yorker pull quote, not a banner ad.

In a feed where every other AI tool's ad is a glassmorphic
gradient with a UI screenshot, this is distinctive by
subtraction.
