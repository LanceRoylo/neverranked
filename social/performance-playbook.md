# Social Performance Playbook

What actually drives stop-scroll, eye-catch, info-density, and
conversion on LinkedIn and Instagram in 2025-2026. Cross-referenced
from Sprout Social, Buffer, Later, plus established
performance-marketing fundamentals. Tuned for Never Ranked's
specific category (B2B + small-business AEO services).

This is the rule layer. Every post built in this directory should
pass these checks before it ships.

Re-verify quarterly. Algorithm preferences shift fast.

---

## The four conversion goals, in priority order

1. **Stop the thumb.** A post that does not stop the scroll has no
   chance to inform or convert. Every other goal is downstream of
   this one.
2. **Reward the stop.** The first line, first 3 seconds, or first
   visual beat must deliver something the scroller can use, feel,
   or argue with. If the stop produces "huh, ok" instead of
   "wait, what?" we have failed.
3. **Lead them through.** Once they have stopped, the body of the
   post earns continued attention with specificity, story, or
   data. Each line earns the next line.
4. **Convert.** A clear, low-pressure path to the next step. For
   Never Ranked: the free scan at check.neverranked.com (top of
   funnel) or the audit (mid-funnel).

If a post does not pass step 1, none of the rest matter. Most
brand social fails here.

## Stop-scroll mechanics

### Visual stop-scroll patterns that work

- **One massive number on dark.** Counterintuitively high stop-rate
  because the brain notices the violation of typical feed density.
  Best Never Ranked example: "1.2%" or "15.1" in oversized Playfair
  italic gold on black.
- **Two-column comparison on dark.** Side-by-side numbers or
  before/after. The contrast triggers the pattern-recognition
  reflex.
- **Receipts (citation cards).** Engine name + actual prompt +
  cited domain. Reads like a screenshot of evidence, not an ad.
  Audience leans in to verify.
- **Italic Playfair mid-sentence on dark.** "Until *now.*" "Is
  *over.*" "*Per plan.*" The italic gold is a signature that
  works as a brand asset by itself.
- **Negative space.** Most of the canvas empty. Counterintuitively
  beats density, the brain registers "this is different" before
  it parses content.

### Visual patterns to refuse (will be ignored)

- Stock photography of people at laptops
- Generic SaaS gradient hero with floating UI
- Bento grids of icons with three-word labels
- "Trusted by" logo soup
- Pastel illustrations
- Charts where the data point is not the focus

## Hook patterns (the first line / first frame)

LinkedIn and Instagram both truncate captions after roughly 125-140
characters before the "see more" cut. The hook MUST land in that
window. The visual hook (image first beat) has even less room, the
viewer is already scrolling past.

### Hook archetypes that consistently win

**1. The contradiction hook** (our strongest pattern)
- Pattern: state two facts that should not be true together.
- Example: "An old friend ranks number one in his category on
  Google. His estimated traffic is zero."
- Why it works: brain stops to resolve the apparent paradox.

**2. The anti-conventional-wisdom hook**
- Pattern: name a thing the audience believes, then dismantle it.
- Example: "The SEO industry has been optimizing for the wrong
  98.8 percent of clicks for two years."
- Why it works: triggers the "wait, that can't be right" reflex.

**3. The receipt-up-front hook**
- Pattern: open with a specific stat or named outcome.
- Example: "Hawaii Theatre went from AEO score 45 to 95 in 24
  hours. 36 schemas in one work session."
- Why it works: specificity signals real work, not theory.

**4. The confession hook**
- Pattern: name something uncomfortable, true, and slightly
  vulnerable.
- Example: "I built Never Ranked because the SEO agency I was
  paying was sending me ranking reports for keywords nobody
  searches anymore."
- Why it works: vulnerability + insider perspective + the implicit
  promise that the rest of the post is honest.

**5. The question hook (use sparingly)**
- Pattern: a question the audience has been asking themselves.
- Example: "Why is your Google ranking holding while your traffic
  is dropping?"
- Why it works: only works when the question is already in their
  head. Otherwise it reads as filler.

### Hook patterns to refuse

- "Excited to announce..." (instant skip)
- "Let me share a few thoughts on..." (instant skip)
- "In today's fast-paced world..." (instant skip and a small
  internal prayer)
- Generic stat without context ("90% of marketers...")
- Hook that telegraphs the punchline ("Here's why X is bad")

## Caption structure (after the hook)

### LinkedIn structure (works for both personal and company page)

```
[HOOK, first 1-2 lines, must work before the "see more" cut]

[BLANK LINE]

[CONTEXT, 2-4 lines, the story or specific data]

[BLANK LINE]

[INSIGHT, 2-3 lines, the reframe / what this actually means]

[BLANK LINE]

[CTA, 1-2 lines, soft, action-oriented]

[BLANK LINE]

[QUESTION, optional but powerful, drives comments]
```

Every paragraph should be 1-3 lines max. Long paragraphs do not
get read on mobile feed. Visual rhythm matters as much as content.

### Instagram structure

```
[HOOK, first ~125 characters, lands before "more" cut]

[BLANK LINE]

[BODY, 4-8 lines, story or data. Tighter than LinkedIn.]

[BLANK LINE]

[CTA, usually "Link in bio" plus context]

[BLANK LINE]

[HASHTAGS, 5-8 on their own line]
```

IG captions can be longer than people think. The constraint is
information density per line, not total length.

## Format selection by goal

| Goal | LinkedIn | Instagram |
|---|---|---|
| **Maximum reach** | Single image post (4:5 portrait) or document carousel PDF | Reels (1080×1920, original audio if possible) |
| **Maximum dwell time** | Document carousel (1200×1500 PDF, up to 12 slides) | Carousel feed post (1080×1350, 3-7 slides) |
| **Maximum shares (the top IG signal)** | Strong text-led posts with a quotable line | Reels with a "send this to a friend who..." moment |
| **Comment-driven engagement** | Single image + question at the end | Carousel with a controversial slide that begs a reply |
| **Direct conversion** | Single image + scan/audit CTA in caption + bio link | Carousel last slide = explicit CTA + link sticker on story reshare |

### Why we usually default to single image for both

- Lowest production cost per post
- Visual templates we already have (the four signature treatments)
- Reusable across LinkedIn AND Instagram at 4:5 portrait
- Forces caption discipline (no "but the carousel will explain it")

### When to actually upgrade

- **Document carousel on LinkedIn** when the post needs 4+ data
  points or steps. The format LinkedIn rewards most generously
  right now per Buffer's data: carousels get 3x the engagement
  of video and 6x the engagement of text-only.
- **Carousel on Instagram** for explainers (e.g. the 5 schema
  gaps every Hawaii restaurant has). Each gap = one slide.
- **Reels on Instagram** when the message has motion in it: a
  schema deploy in real time, a citation appearing on a dashboard,
  the constellation network animating. Reels are >50% of all
  Instagram ad inventory now per Sprout, the algorithm pushes
  them hardest.

## The signature visual treatments (Never Ranked specific)

Four visual templates we should reuse, not reinvent:

### A. Single big number
One stat. Massive Playfair italic gold on dark. Mono caption.
Best for: stat-as-hero posts, "1.2%", "15.1", "45 → 95."
Files: `hero-instagram.png` from this post is the canonical
example.

### B. Two-number contrast
Two stats side by side or stacked. Contrast carries the message.
Best for: paradoxes, before/after, comparisons.
Files: this post's `hero-instagram.png` shows the stacked vertical
treatment. The earlier `hero.png` shows the horizontal LinkedIn
treatment.

### C. Citation receipts
Five engine cards (or one zoomed in). Engine name, prompt, cited
domain, CITED/MISSING tag. Reads like a screenshot of evidence.
Best for: "what we track" posts, weekly receipt series.
File spec: `social/posts/2026-05-05-citation-receipt/hero-prompt.md`
has the brief.

### D. Constellation
Brand Data + Website Content + Knowledge Graph + Schema → Your
Brand → ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews, Claude,
Copilot. Animated for Reels, static for feed.
Best for: explaining what AEO actually does mechanically.
Already exists in the live website hero.

If a post needs a fifth visual archetype, it had better justify
why none of the four work. Templates compound, repeated patterns
build the brand.

## Hashtag rules (current truth)

### LinkedIn
- **0-3 hashtags** maximum.
- Place at the end of the caption, on their own line.
- Per Buffer: hashtags are "a nice to have, not a need to have."
- For Never Ranked, when used: `#AEO #AnswerEngineOptimization #AISearch`
- Personal Lance posts: usually zero. The algorithm does not
  reward them on personal posts as of late 2025.

### Instagram
- **5-8 hashtags** ideal.
- Per Later: "5 or 10 hashtags is better than zero." Quality over
  volume. The old "use all 30" advice is dead.
- Mix broad + niche + branded.
- Place on their own line at the very end of the caption.
- Standard set:
  `#AEO #AnswerEngineOptimization #AISearch #SEO2026 #SchemaMarkup #SmallBusinessSEO #AIcitations #NeverRanked`
- Vertical-specific posts add the vertical: `#RestaurantMarketing`,
  `#NonprofitMarketing`, `#HawaiiBusiness` (for Hawaii Theatre etc).

## Posting cadence + timing

### LinkedIn
- **Personal:** 3-4 posts per week is the sweet spot. More than
  daily and engagement per post drops sharply.
- **Company page:** 2-3 per week. The algorithm does not feed
  company pages aggressively, so consistency over volume.
- **Best windows:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7am-10am Pacific (covers
  East Coast morning) and 11am-2pm Pacific (covers West Coast
  morning). Per Buffer: "Anything between 7am and 4pm on weekdays
  had the highest engagement rate."
- **Avoid:** Friday afternoon, weekends, holidays.

### Instagram
- **Brand account:** 3-5 feed posts per week + 5-15 stories per
  week. Reels at 1-3 per week.
- **Best windows:** Wednesday-Friday, 11am-1pm and 7pm-9pm.
- **Stories drive feed reach.** Posting a story right after a feed
  post, with a sticker that re-invites engagement, lifts the feed
  post's reach in the first hour. Critical lever.

## The first-hour engagement loop (do not skip)

Both algorithms heavily weight engagement in the first hour. A
post that gets traction in the first 60 minutes gets distributed
to a wider audience for the next 24 hours. A post that does not
gets killed.

### What to do in the first hour
1. **Reply to every single comment within 5 minutes.** The
   algorithm reads "post owner is engaged" as a signal to push
   the post further.
2. **Reply with a question.** Drives a follow-up comment. Comment
   threads (multi-reply) count more than single comments.
3. **Story re-share** (Instagram) of the feed post inside the
   first 30 minutes.
4. **Share to relevant LinkedIn groups** (where you are a member
   and it is genuinely on-topic).
5. **Tag 1-2 specific people** in the post or comments where
   they would naturally have something to say (NEVER mass tag).

### Conversion mechanics by platform

**LinkedIn personal:**
- Soft mention of `check.neverranked.com` or `neverranked.com` in
  the caption (parenthetical works, see the rank-zero post)
- Tag the Never Ranked company page in the caption (helps the
  company page get incidental reach)
- For mid-funnel: end with "DM if you want me to look at yours"
  for prospects who self-identify

**LinkedIn company page:**
- Same as personal but more direct CTAs are tolerated
- Link in first comment is the older move; link in caption is the
  current one (LinkedIn softened the link-suppression rules in
  late 2025)

**Instagram brand account:**
- Bio link at `check.neverranked.com` for the day of any post
  driving to the scanner
- Story re-share with link sticker pointing to the same URL
- For mid-funnel: a story poll sticker that surfaces self-identified
  prospects ("Has your business been cited by an AI engine in the
  last week? YES / I have no idea")
- DM the YES voters with a specific follow-up

**Instagram personal:**
- Lance's personal IG is lower priority but the same patterns work
- Bio link, story re-share, occasional cross-post

## Conversion benchmarks to set expectations

These are realistic, not aspirational:

- **LinkedIn personal post engagement rate:** 3-7% is good for a
  founder with an audience. 1-3% is normal. <1% means the hook
  failed.
- **Instagram brand engagement rate:** 0.48% is the platform
  benchmark per Sprout. 1-2% is good. 3%+ is excellent.
- **Click-through to bio link / external URL:** 0.5-2% of
  impressions. A 50,000-impression post that drives 200-500 scans
  to check.neverranked.com is a win.
- **Scan to audit conversion:** unknown for Never Ranked yet, but
  industry baseline for B2B free-tool-to-paid-engagement is 1-5%.
  Track this over the first 90 days of posting.

If a post lands well below these numbers, the playbook is failing
somewhere. Diagnose: was it the hook, the visual, the time of day,
the format choice? Iterate the next post.

## Anti-patterns (what fails consistently)

- Posting the same content type 3 times in a row (algorithm shows
  fatigue)
- Asking for engagement directly ("Like if you agree", both
  platforms penalize this now)
- Long opening paragraphs that bury the hook below the "see more"
  cut
- Using the brand voice rules but ignoring the visual rules (or
  vice versa)
- Posting a beautifully designed visual with a phoned-in caption
  (or vice versa)
- Hopping on a trend that contradicts the brand voice (Lance does
  not need to do trending audio if it does not match the editorial
  positioning)
- Not replying to comments in the first hour
- Generic CTAs at the end ("DM us to learn more")

## The five-second pre-publish check

Before any post ships, run through these five questions. If any
answer is "no" or "not sure," delay or fix.

1. **Does the first line / first visual beat stop the scroll?**
   (Read it cold like you have never seen it. Is it a "huh" or a
   "wait, what"?)
2. **Does it pass the swap test?** Replace "Never Ranked" with any
   competitor name. Does the post still make sense? If yes, it is
   too generic. Add specificity.
3. **Does it name the creative lever?** (Reframe, Specificity,
   Confession, Tension Hold, Format Surprise, Visible Mechanic.
   See voice-quickref.md.)
4. **Does it have a specific number, name, or quoted phrase?**
   Specificity is the single biggest predictor of stop-rate for
   evidence-based brands like ours.
5. **Is the conversion path clear and low-pressure?** Free scan,
   audit-on-me, soft mention. Not "BOOK A DEMO NOW."

If five for five, ship it. Reply to every comment in the first
hour. Re-share to story (Instagram) within 30 minutes.
